In today's dynamic business landscape, technology executives are increasingly called upon to

deliver transformational outcomes during mergers, acquisitions, and divestitures—often under

aggressive timelines and with extraordinary expectations. To succeed, it's no longer enough to

simply integrate systems or rationalize infrastructure. True success lies in understanding and

driving the strategic business outcomes behind the merger itself.

The TBM Framework provides an indispensable blueprint for navigating these high-stakes

initiatives. It enables technology leaders to move beyond surface-level integration activities and

drive tangible, strategic value—becoming critical partners to the CEO, CFO, and Board in realizing

the goals of the transaction.

Connected Standards: A Common Language for

Integration

TBM doesn't just align technology and finance—it also brings a common language that

accelerates integration across organizations.

By leveraging connected standards (such as NIST for cybersecurity profiles, ITIL for operational

practices, or FinOps for cloud cost management), technology teams can:

 Quickly assess risk and maturity across both organizations

 Facilitate joint conversations around outcomes and value drivers

 Provide leadership teams with an added layer of confidence in technology integration

plans

Standards provide bridges that help reconcile different processes, tools, and risk postures

across different organizations enabling faster, more confident decision-making.

TBM Foundations: Building Toward a Unified Future

TBM’s Foundations layer—Data, Tools, Methods, Roles, and Change Management (OCM)—

provides critical integration guidance:

 Data: Don’t simply migrate. Define a three-year target state data architecture that
supports the combined organization's strategy and build toward it methodically.

 Tools: Assess technology management tools on both sides; decide which systems to

sunset, consolidate, or integrate.

 Methods and Roles: Blend best practices and realign roles across both organizations to

reflect new delivery models.

 Change Management (OCM): Communicate a common vision, use TBM insights to

reinforce transparency, and recognize that cultural integration is just as critical as technical

integration.

TBM doesn’t just support these foundational activities—it amplifies them, giving leaders the

insights needed to manage complexity and navigate change with confidence.

About the TBM Council

Founded in 2012, the Technology Business Management (TBM) Council is a nonprofit

business entity dedicated to advancing the discipline of TBM through education, standards,

and collaboration. Governed by an independent board of both global and regional business

technology leaders, this diverse group represents some of the world’s most innovative

companies, including Mastercard, Wells Fargo, State Farm Insurance, Nike, Stanley Black &

Decker, Equifax, ANZ Group, Commonwealth Bank of Australia, Adidas, Mercedes Benz, and

more. The TBM Council provides best practices for leaders to leverage so they can react

quickly to changing market dynamics and optimize cloud and agile strategies to deliver on

business objectives.
