## Taxonmy Extension Overview
| Overview of TBM Taxonomy Extensions |
| --- |
| The standard TBM Taxonomy has been developed and is maintained by the TBM Council's Standards Committee. This committee is chaired by a TBM Council board member and is comprised of senior TBM professionals from a variety of industries. However, the committee and its TBM Taxonomy and Metrics subcommittee is not adequately staffed to develop a taxonomy that meets all the needs of TBM programs in all industries. |

| In order to address these broader industry needs, the TBM Council supports extending the TBM Taxonomy through a formal mechanism (TBM Council Industry Workgroups) that will help preserve certain attributes of the standard taxonomy, such as its support of industry benchmarking, while leveraging the contribution of Council members that may not sit on the Standards Committee or any subcommittees. |
| NaN |
| The overarching principle behind TBM Taxonomy Extensions is that the standard TBM Taxonomy remains intact. The majority of the taxonomy, from the bottom layer Cost Pools and Sub-Pools to the IT Towers and even most of its Products & Services (especially the technical services of Infrastructure, Platform, and Delivery), can be applied to IT organizations in every industry. However, TBM Taxonomy Extensions will add to these standard elements, primarily at the Business Services layer of the TBM Taxonomy, to address unique industry requirements. |
| NaN |
| Therefore, the primary purpose of each industry vertical workgroup is to create a Business Services taxonomy specific to the vertical. |
| NaN |
| In this way, TBM Taxonomy Extensions are additions to the standard TBM Taxonomy v3.0, not changes to it. For example, a healthcare provider would still use many of the standard Shared Services that are defined by the TBM Taxonomy, such as Financial Planning, Fixed Assets, Payroll, and so on, but would also use many (and possibly all) of the Business Services defined by the Health Care workgroup. These include Service Categories like Clinical Services and Patient Support & Engagement and Service Names such as Cardiology, Oncology, Food Services and Patient Portal. |
| NaN |
| Over time, the TBM Council anticipates that its members will define dozens of TBM Taxonomy Extensions using the TBM TEK. As the Council and its workgroup members gain experience in doing so, we will continue to improve the TEK, including these guidelines. |
