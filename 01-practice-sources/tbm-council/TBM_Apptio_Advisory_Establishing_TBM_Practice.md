# 1. TBM Introduction
TBM Connects Practices for Technology Value Management

![](Picture3.jpg)
TBM spans ITFM, SPM, and FinOps and other related practices, providing a unified approach to managing technology investments across the enterprise—from on-prem, to cloud, to highly skilled tech labor.

Taking a comprehensive approach to technology investment management makes it easier to stay aligned with corporate goals and translate investments into clear business value.

TBM elevates technology to the level of corporate strategy by translating tech spend into a language the rest of the business can understand via a taxonomy for identifying and allocating tech costs and investments.

### Notes:
So what is TBM?

Technology Business Management (TBM) is a discipline that improves business outcomes by giving organizations a consistent way to translate technology investments to business value by defining the tools, processes, data, and people needed to manage the business of technology. Based on a set of standard taxonomy that can be used by technology, finance and business leaders, TBM enables organizations to react quickly to changing market dynamics, make data-driven decisions, and align organizations around a common business objective.

TBM is a discipline: that means it is a practice adopted by organizations to leverage decision-making tools, including both a framework and taxonomy for organizing technology investment information.

# How Data Quality Impacts Allocations and Value
The ability to accurately allocate costs, enabling better insights to make better decisions and therefore drive greater value are closely aligned to data quality. However, poor data quality does not mean you cannot allocate costs effectively and start receiving value.  TBM uses a Good, Better, Best approach to allocating costs from all levels of data quality.

Take the following example of allocating support labor costs to Application TCO:

BEST
Application support labor data now available from reliable and accurate weekly timesheets
Agree a Consumptive  allocation of labor costs with application owners, based on the actual time spent
VALUE

As data quality increases, so does the ability to allocate costs accurately,  therefore enabling better insights & decisions, driving greater value
BETTER
Application support labor data now available from the monthly resource assignments
Agree an Attribute to allocate labor costs with application owners, in this case employee assignment

ALLOCATION ACCURACY
GOOD
Poor quality or lack of existence of application support labor data – timesheets incomplete
Agree an Assumption of labor cost split with application owners – x% app1, y% app2 etc.

# What Do We Mean by Data Quality?
| Quality Attribute | Common Challenge | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Availability Does the source data exist? | Source data sometimes does not exist when the org has never implemented TBM. In most cases, however, data exists but is not in a format that is useful. | IT maintains asset (often CMDB), service desk, and monitoring tools but the data remains siloed within IT ops. |
| Timeliness How long does it taketo obtain the data? | Access to data is often impeded by data transformation and adjustment requirements, such as end-of-period adjustments and ETL processes. | Financial data is delayed by the monthly closing process where adjusting journal entries are made and other transactions are booked. |
| Completeness Does the data source provide all the necessary information? | Completeness generally refers to two conditions: (1) data source does not contain needed fields; (2) data source represents only a portion of your reality (i.e., only part of the scope). | Service desk tickets fail to document all of the personnel involved in fulfilling each request. An asset inventory includes your data center servers but not the servers deployed outside of the data center. |
| Accuracy How trustworthy is the source data? | Poor controls over source data and a lack of routine processes often contribute to data integrity issues. | Asset inventory data is constantly out-of-date because there is no routine process for maintaining it. |
| Referential Integrity Can data sources be joined or related? | Organizations often fail to create and enforce naming standards across all of their source applications for TBM. | The fixed assets system uses a different identifier for servers than is used by IT, making it difficult to allocate (assign) depreciation costs. |

# Prioritization of Use Cases – Key elements to consider
The prioritization of use cases is owned by the TBM Advisory Group and facilitated by the TBM Office.
Run use case prioritisation workshops on a quarterly basis (at a minimum)
Ensure that no more than 25% of the total use case count are prioritized as High, 25% as Medium, and 50% are Low.  This will ensure a key focus on High priority use cases.
Take into consideration the value opportunity types and ensure there is an appropriate balance of cost reduction, cost avoidance and management.
Do no prioritize use cases solely on financial value return.  The TBM Advisory Group should also consider the following during the prioritization process:

# 6 Hallmarks of success for a TBM adoption
Insights
The role of the TBM Office must change from a reporting office to insights hunting. Only active working with the new data and insights can trigger actions and decisions to  achieve the envisaged values.
TBM Governance & Office

Successful TBM programs are driven top-down incl. proper governance and sponsorship, e.g. a steering committee, an advisory group or an exec sponsor, providing guidance and eliminating road blockers.  TBM Office ensures efficient operations. Both supporting ongoing adoption
Roadmap

The TBM roadmaps and/or a program charter provides guidance, serves as a formal commitment aligning the TBM team, the stakeholders and leadership with the business goals. Regular or event driven reviews ensuring ongoing alignment.
Processes

TBM outcome must be incorporated in business processes to reshape decision-making, accountability, and financial transparency. Next to output-oriented processes, TBM needs well designed operating processes.

Evolution
A TBM implementation growth over time – data, reports and functions, technology & methods are evolving, also with new trends (e.g. agile, cloud) which needs not only an open mindset, but also quality insurance, and solid change management
Finance & technology partnership
TBM depends on collaboration between technology and finance leaders. TBM is not shadow finance but result oriented and enhances pure finance data. Partnership is essential, as a unified voice between finance and tech leaders builds trust
