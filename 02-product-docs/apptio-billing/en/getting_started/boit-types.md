# Billing Standard Types

A Billing Standard can be one of the following types:

- Cost-based Billing Standard
- Price-based Billing Standard
- Budget-based Billing Standard
- Hybrid Billing Standard

## Cost-based Billing Standard

The cost-based Billing Standard calculates a total cost of ownership (TCO) of IT services, and
then allocates that cost out to the business units. The TCO can be calculated in any way that fits
the business. It could be as simple as a spreadsheet uploaded to the application to populate the
costs, or it could pull the cost from Apptio Cost Transparency. The allocation can be based on a
variety of different factors such as the number of employees in a business unit.

In the Apptio model, price is equal to the unit cost of a service.

In this type of Billing Standard, no cost adjustments or incentives are used.

## Price-based Billing Standard

The price-based Billing Standard also calculates the total cost of ownership of IT services, but
it does not expose that cost to the business units. Instead, this type of bill publishes a price
that is different from the actual cost. The price of each service is tracked separately from the
cost.

Use this type of Billing Standard to implement incentive-based pricing and to use price to change
behaviors.

## Budget-based Billing Standard

The budget-based Billing Standard develops the Billing Standard during the budget cycles. As the
budget is developed, the prices in the Billing Standard use a planned rate. The planned rate can be
calculated in any way that fits the business. It could be as simple as a spreadsheet uploaded to the
application, or it could pull data from Apptio. In turn, the planned rate can be based on cost or
price.

In this type of Billing Standard, the communication out to the business units informs them in
advance about the amount of their budget that will be allocated to IT services each month.

## Hybrid Billing Standard

The hybrid Billing Standard uses multiple types to calculate costs.
