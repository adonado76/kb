# Key benefits

At a high level, Billing helps you:

- **Make Technology spend visible and explainable**
  - Show **who is paying for what**, using a consistent catalog of services, products, or
    offerings.
  - Present charges in business language (services and outcomes), not raw infrastructure
    or GL codes.
  - Provide drill-downs from summary views into the underlying drivers so Admins,
    Analysts, and Service or Product Owners can explain bills with confidence.
- **Connect cost, consumption, and price**
  - Link **Costing outputs**, **consumption volumes**, and **rates** into a
    single PxQ view.
  - Compare cost per unit, price per unit, and, where modeled, planned cost or
    budget.
  - Support conversations about:
    - Under- or over-recovery
    - Unit rate movements over time
    - Where optimization efforts should focus
- **Reduce manual effort and spreadsheet risk**
  - Replace ad hoc billing spreadsheets with **repeatable models** and **standard
    reports**.
  - Automate:
    - Monthly data refreshes and model runs
    - Core QA checks and exception reporting
    - Journal exports where chargeback is in scope
  - Decrease reliance on single individuals maintaining fragile manual logic.
- **Enable role-appropriate views of Technology charges**
  - Give **Admins and Analysts** the tools to configure, run, and validate the billing
    cycle.
  - Give **Service and Product Owners** clear views of charges for their offerings and
    consumers.
  - Give **Finance** journal-ready outputs and reconciliation views to the general
    ledger.
  - Give **Senior Leaders and stakeholders** concise dashboards for trends, top
    consumers, and key domains like cloud or projects.

- **Support different billing maturity levels**
  - **Billing Essentials**
    - Focuses on reliable PxQ showback and straightforward chargeback.
    - Suitable when the primary goal is transparency and behavioral change, not complex
      domain analysis.
  - **Billing Standard**
    - Adds richer domain views (cloud, applications, infrastructure, projects, legal
      entities) and advanced topics such as unit-rate optimization, variance analysis, and
      transfer pricing.
    - Supports more sophisticated financial and operational analysis without losing
      traceability back to the same core services and consumers.
- **Strengthen governance and trust in Technology charges**
  - Aligns with TBM concepts and the Costing model so terminology and structures are
    consistent across reports and processes.
  - Encourages clear ownership of services, consumers, units, and rates.
  - Provides a repeatable audit trail from raw data through allocations to final charges
    and journals, making Technology billing defensible to Finance, auditors, and
    stakeholders.
