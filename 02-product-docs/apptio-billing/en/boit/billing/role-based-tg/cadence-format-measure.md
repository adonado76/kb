# Training, Cadence, and Formats

Once initial onboarding is done, training should not stop. At a minimum:

- **Quarterly or semi-annual refresher sessions**
  - New features or report changes.
  - Updated patterns or recipes.
  - Lessons learned from recent cycles.
- **New joiner onboarding**
  - Brief, role-specific sessions for new Admins, Analysts, Service Owners, or Finance
    staff.
  - Reuse content and exercises from earlier phases.
- **Micro-guides and job aids**
  - Short, focused documents or pages:
    - How to read your bill.
    - How to export journals.
    - How to investigate a spike.

## Measuring training effectiveness

If training is working, you should see:

- Fewer basic “where do I click” questions over time.
- More targeted questions about real patterns and decisions.
- Fewer last minute firefights around billing cycles.
- Stable or shrinking time spent per period on reruns and manual corrections.

Simple ways to measure:

- Track number and type of Billing-related support tickets or questions.
- Ask a small set of standard questions in periodic surveys, for example:
  - “I know where to find the Billing reports I need.”
  - “I understand what my charges represent.”
  - “I know who to contact when something looks off.”

Use that feedback to refine which topics need deeper coverage and where new job aids or
examples would help.
