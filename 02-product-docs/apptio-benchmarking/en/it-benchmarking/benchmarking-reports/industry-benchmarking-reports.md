# Industry Benchmark reports in Interactive Benchmarking

The Industry Benchmark reports answer “Are we broadly in line with peers at the enterprise
level?”

They provide access to 37 industries to compare benchmark metrics to improve executive
storytelling with simple, credible visuals, identify where deeper investigation is worth the effort,
and get trend context to support planning.

## IT spend as a percentage of (Organizational) revenue

![IT spend as a percentage of (Organizational) revenue](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/IT-spend-as-percentage.png)

Practical benefits:

- Select a region, revenue band, or global industry distribution to benchmark your reality (not a
  generic average).
- See your position, not just a number.
- Separate structural drivers from execution issues.

## IT spend as a percentage of (Organizational) OpEx

![IT spend as a percentage of (Organizational) OpEx](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/it-spend-opex.png)

Practical benefits:

- Normalize IT spend against how “expensive” the business is to run.
- Get a clearer read on operating efficiency and IT intensity.
- Triage where to dig deeper next.

## Percent OpEx spend (of IT compared to total spend)

![Percent OpEx spend (of IT compared to total spend)](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/percentage-opex-spend.png)

Practical benefits:

- Understand your IT spend mix, OpEx-heavy vs CapEx-heavy.
- Spot operating model shifts like cloud migration and XaaS adoption.
- Improve budgeting and forecasting accuracy.
- Benchmark your OpEx/CapEx posture against the right peer context.
- See whether your position is merely “below median” or truly abnormal.
- Support decisions on investment strategy and funding approach.

## IT spend per (total Organization) employee

![IT spend per (total Organization) employee](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/it-spend-per-employee.png)

Practical benefits:

- Normalize IT spend in a way executives instantly understand.
- Compare efficiency and “IT intensity” independent of company size.
- Reveal where your operating model is heavier or lighter than peers.

## IT FTE per 100 employees (in Organization)

![IT FTE per 100 employees (in Organization)](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/it-fte.png)

Practical benefits:

- Benchmark staffing intensity in clear views.
- Separate “we spend a lot” from “we staff a lot”.
- Identify sourcing model differences (in-house vs outsourced).

## How to use them

Good questions to ask:

- Are we in a reasonable band, or way outside it?
- If we are high, is that deliberate (for example digital strategy, security, regulatory
  load)?
- If we are low, is that efficiency, underinvestment, or missing cost categories?

Patterns to look for:

- Slightly above median, inside quartile band; usually fine, deserves context, not panic.
- Well above upper quartile; first check scope, if valid, treat as a serious question.
- Below lower quartile; could be efficiency or a sign that key areas are starved.

Use Industry views to:

- Frame conversations with Finance and the board.
- Set the context before you talk about towers and initiatives.

Do not use them alone to justify blanket cost cuts.
