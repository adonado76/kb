# Using Benchmarking Reports

This section is for people who use Benchmarking output, not wire it up.   
Audience:

- TBMAs and ITFM analysts who build decks, answer “why” questions, and prep content
- Sponsors and execs such as CIO, CTO, CFO, VP Finance, VP Infrastructure
- Resource Tower owners who want to know if their area is “good, bad, or weird”

If you own configuration details, see the [Configuration Guide section](../home.html#benchmarking__config_guide). In this section, we focus on how to read and use the
reports.

**What these reports are (and are not)**   
Benchmarking reports are designed to:

- Show where you stand versus peers
- Highlight which areas deserve attention
- Provide drill paths so you can move from “signal” to “investigation”

  
They are not:

- A replacement for internal financial reporting
- A guarantee that peers have identical scope or operating models
- A checklist that tells you “cut spending here by 12 percent”

  
Treat them as structured input to decisions, not as an autopilot.
