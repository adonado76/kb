# Practical usage scenarios

Here are a few high value ways to use the reports without overcomplicating things.

**Defending a budget that looks “too high”**  
The situation…Finance or the board
thinks IT spend is high and wants cuts.

How to use the reports:

1. Start with an **IT Spend** view:
   - IT spend as a percentage of Resource Tower spend Cost Pool compared to benchmarks
   - Show where you sit relative to your Archetype.

     ![Costing’s Industry & OpEx Benchmarks report’s IT Spend tab](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/benchmark-it-spend-tab.png)
2. Move to **Application Distribution**:
   - IT spend percentage broken down to Resource Sub-Towers within the “Application” Resource Tower
     and compared to benchmarks
   - Show where you sit relative to your Archetype.
3. Only then, if needed, go into **Infrastructure** and benchmark-enabled Costing for specific
   towers.

Goal: shift the conversation from “reduce by 10 percent” to “do we agree this is the right
operating model, given our strategy and risk profile?”

**Targeting a cost optimization initiative**  
The situation…you need to find
credible savings without random across-the-board cuts.

How to use the reports:

1. Use **IT OpEx** and **Infrastructure** views to find outlier towers or cost pools.
2. For a chosen outlier, open a **benchmark-enabled Costing** report.
3. Drill to identify drivers:
   - Specific vendors
   - Specific regions or data centers
   - Specific services or platforms.
4. Turn these into initiatives:
   - License optimization
   - Rationalization and consolidation
   - Sourcing changes
   - Hardware refresh or tiering.

Goal: Link each initiative to a visible benchmark gap; it becomes much easier to defend and
track.

**Planning tower transformation**  
The situation…you plan a multi-year change for a
tower such as End User, Network, or Storage.

How to use the reports:

1. Use **Infrastructure** benchmarks to baseline current unit cost and FTE efficiency.
2. Define a realistic **target band** based on quartiles, not the absolute minimum.
3. Work with the tower owner to identify:
   - What architecture, tooling, or sourcing changes would move you toward that band,
   - What investment is required
4. Track progress annually using the same benchmark metrics.

Goal: use benchmarks to anchor expectations and show progress, not to chase perfection.
