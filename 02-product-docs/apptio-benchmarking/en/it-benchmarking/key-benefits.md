# Key Benefits

You can use Benchmarking to deliver value across several themes.

- **Know where you stand vs peers** 
  - Compare total IT spend and key ratios to peer organizations
  - Compare cost structure by Cost Pool and Resource Tower to understand your operating model
  - Review unit costs and FTE efficiencies at Resource Tower and Sub-tower levels

    This helps
    answer questions such as…
    - Are our data center unit costs high or low compared to similar organizations?
    - Is our spend mix skewed toward labor, vendors, or technology relative to peers?
    - Are we over or under investing in specific towers?
- **Defend and justify IT spend** 
  - Show that costs are within a reasonable range of benchmarks given your scale and complexity
  - Demonstrate where you are already efficient and where you intentionally spend more for
    resilience, experience, or innovation

    This supports budget cycles, investment cases, and steering
    committees by moving the discussion from opinions to data.
- **Identify and prioritize optimization opportunities** 
  - Highlight areas where your cost or unit cost is significantly above benchmark ranges
  - Use drill-through into Costing to see the specific drivers behind outliers
  - Decide whether gaps are due to mapping, scope differences, or genuine inefficiency

    This
    avoids random cost-cutting and focuses efforts where there is measurable benchmark variance.
- **Set informed targets and track progress** 
  - Use benchmark values and distributions to set realistic but ambitious targets
  - Track actuals vs benchmark over time at Cost Pool, Resource Tower, and unit-cost levels
  - Monitor progress as initiatives complete and sourcing models change

    Benchmarking turns
    one-time “are we high or low” conversations into an ongoing performance management process.
- **Maintain trust in IT cost data** 
  - Validate TBM mappings by comparing your cost distributions to peer distributions
  - Use differences to spot potential mapping issues or missing scope
  - Provide transparency down to line-item detail to prove that reported costs are defensible

    When stakeholders challenge numbers, you can back them with both internal traceability and
    external benchmarks.
