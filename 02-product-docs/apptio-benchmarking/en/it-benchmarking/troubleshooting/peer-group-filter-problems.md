# Peer group and filter problems

**Metrics disappear when filters are applied**

Symptom  
A metric shows up with broad filters, but disappears when you narrow the peer
group.  
Likely causes

- Peer group falls below the minimum required sample size for that metric
- Too few organizations in that slice report the metric
- Dataset is suppressing results to maintain participant anonymity

What to check

1. Remove filters one at a time and see when the metric comes back.
2. Note which filter (industry subsegment, region, size band) is causing thin samples.
3. Use a slightly broader peer group for any metric that regularly disappears.

Short answer you can send  
When we filter that narrowly, there aren’t enough
organizations to show a reliable or anonymous benchmark, so the metric is suppressed. Widening the
peer group slightly restores the benchmark with a solid sample size.

**Stakeholder disputes peer group choice**

Symptom  
Resource Tower owner or exec argues the chosen peer group is not
appropriate.  
Likely causes

- Industry classification doesn’t match how they see the business
- Size band or region feels off to them
- They are reacting to an uncomfortable gap, not a genuinely bad peer group

What to check

1. Confirm how your organization is classified in profile (industry, size, region) and why.
2. Test a small number of alternate peer groups and see how much results move.
3. Check that their requested group isn’t cherry-picking (for example small subset that makes you
   look good).

Short answer you can send  
We set the peer group to match our primary industry, size
band, and region. We can test alternate groups, but we need to pick one definition and stick with it
for decisions, not change peers every time a number is uncomfortable.
