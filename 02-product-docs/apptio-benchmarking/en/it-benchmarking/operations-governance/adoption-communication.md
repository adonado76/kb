# Adoption and communication patterns

If you do not intentionally socialize how to use Benchmarking, people will either ignore it or
weaponize it.

## Standard communication assets

Create and maintain a small core set:

- One-page Benchmarking overview
  - What it is
  - Who owns it
  - When we use it (planning, QBRs, Resource Tower reviews)
- A couple of real examples
  - Case where a benchmark helped defend spend
  - Case where a benchmark highlighted a realistic optimization
- Mini FAQ (exec-facing)
  - What is our peer group?
  - How precise are these metrics?
  - Does above median always mean bad?

These do more for adoption than another 30-page deck.

## Training for executives vs analysts

Executives:

- Focus on reading charts and understanding peer groups and limitations
- Emphasize how benchmarks guide *questions*, not dictate *answers*
- Avoid TBM configuration details

Analysts / TBMAs:

- Train on drill paths, data lineage, and mapping decisions
- Give them scripts for common objections
- Walk through **[Troubleshooting &
  FAQ](../home.html#benchmarking__faq_tr)** scenarios: blank charts, weird values, peer disputes

Goal: execs trust and use the outputs; analysts can explain and defend them.
