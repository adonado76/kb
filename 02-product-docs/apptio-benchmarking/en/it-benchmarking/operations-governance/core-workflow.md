# Core workflows

Here is where Benchmarking shows up in real work.

**New program rollout**  
 When: Benchmarking is newly implemented, or a new
business unit is brought into scope.

Steps:

1. Benchmarking Owner validates core configuration and basic views.  
   **NOTE:** See
   **[Quickstart](../home.html#benchmarking__quickstart)** & **[Configuration Guide](../home.html#benchmarking__config_guide)** sections for more
   information.
2. TBM Lead defines the **approved default peer group** for that unit.
3. Benchmarking Owner prepares a concise initial pack of 3–5 views:
   - 1 Industry view (IT spend vs revenue)
   - 1 OpEx structure view
   - 1–2 infra views if available
4. TBM Lead walks through with sponsor (CIO / VP-level):
   - Here is where we stand vs peers
   - Here are the gaps that merit further investigation
5. Capture decisions:
   - Which metrics will be tracked regularly
   - Which towers / areas are priority
   - How often you will revisit benchmarks

Output: a simple 1–2 page “Benchmarking charter” for that unit.

## Annual IT planning

When: Budgeting / planning cycle.  
Steps:

1. By end of 2nd quarter of a calendar year, Benchmarking Owner refreshes Benchmarking
   to the latest full year and validates core metrics.
2. For each major Resource Tower or domain:
   - Review unit costs and OpEx structures vs peers
   - Identify where you are significantly high or low
3. TBM Lead and Finance use this to:
   - Inform efficiency targets (for example “move End User support toward the 60th percentile over 3
     years”)
   - Sanity-check large investment or reduction proposals against relevant benchmarks

Output: targets and planning narratives anchored in external context, not just internal
history.

## Resource Tower optimization initiative

When: You choose a Resource Tower or function for targeted improvement.

Steps:

1. Use infra and OpEx benchmarks to size the gap:
   - Unit cost variance
   - Spend share vs peers
2. Use benchmark-enabled Costing reports to drill into:
   - Cost pools
   - Vendors and contracts
   - Regions and data centers
   - Services or applications
3. Resource Tower Owner and TBM Lead translate gaps into levers:
   - Sourcing or renegotiation
   - Consolidation / retirement
   - Automation and tooling
   - Architecture standardization
4. Track progress annually using the same benchmark metrics and peer definitions.

Output: a Resource Tower improvement plan with specific, benchmark-linked targets and
levers.

## Executive challenge session

When: A senior stakeholder does not trust or like what the benchmarks show.

Steps:

1. Start with **definition and peer group**, not the gap:
   - “We are comparing ourselves to [industry, region, size band], year [N].”
   - “This metric is defined as [numerator / denominator].”
2. Show your position vs quartiles.
3. Immediately follow with internal drill:
   - Use Costing to show the underlying costs and volumes.
   - Clarify inclusions and exclusions.
4. If there is genuine scope or mapping disagreement:
   - Log it as an issue.
   - Agree either to adjust configuration or to document the rationale and keep it.

Output: either a refined configuration or explicit agreement on how to interpret the
metric.
