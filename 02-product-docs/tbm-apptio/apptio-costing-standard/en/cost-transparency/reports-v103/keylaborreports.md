# Key Labor Reports

The table below identifies key reports that are available once you have configured the
Labor component. Use the table as you review the reports to identify the objectives of the reports
as well as the questions addressed by the reports.

| Report | Audience | Objectives | Questions Addressed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Costing Standard - Summary | CIOsIT Management | Understand where IT spend is occurring and quickly identify outliers or unplanned deviations by reviewing the charts.Compare YTD and Annual spend to plan by tower, cost pool, internal vs. external labor and top projects by reviewing the tables.Drill into a summary for a specific tower, cost pool, labor type, or project by clicking an item in one of the tables. | Where am I spending most of my money? Is it reasonable?Where is my spend not on track to meet plan?Do I have areas under budget that balance this overspend? |
| IT Finance - Labor | IT Finance personnel | View labor costs by internal and external personnel by cost center.Understand the labor spend to budget by cost center for the current month, quarter, and YTD. | You can use the information presented on this report to answer the following questions:How much have I spent on labor this year?How am I doing against overall headcount and budget plans?Which department or cost center accounts for my largest labor spend?Where is my biggest variance, by amount?Is action required to mitigate budget risk? |
| Data Dimensions - Labor | TBM administrator | Assess the completeness of data. Completeness is measured as the number of cells in the supporting data sets that have values. For example, a completeness score of 80% indicates that 80% of the cells have values. In general, a score of 80% or above is acceptable.Assess the validity of the data. Validity is determined by comparing the values in the data to a list of acceptable values you uploaded into the application. In general, a score of 80% or above is acceptable.Assess the uniqueness of the data. In general, a score of 80% or above is acceptable. | Is my data complete enough to make informed decisions?Is the data correct? |
