# Labor Review

Use this report to track labor costs and employee headcount across your IT organization,
comparing actual spending and headcount against budgets to identify areas where staffing levels or
costs need adjustment.

This report is designed for use by the following roles:

- IT Financial Controller
- CIO
- HR Leaders
- Cost Center Owners
- Finance Teams

## Key Elements

![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-lr-1.png)![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-lr-2.png)![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-lr-3.png)

| Element | Description |
| --- | --- |
| KPI Cards (1) | Five KPI cards show total spend year to date, labor as a percentage of IT spend year to date, labor headcount, internal headcount, and external headcount. |
| Filter Controls (2) | Filters let you narrow the report by cost center and name, owner, role type, location, and employee type. |
| Time Period Selectors (3) | Use these controls to view data for the current month, current quarter, or year to date. |
| Top 5 Total Spend by Category Chart (4) | A horizontal bar chart shows the top five total spend categories for internal labor and external labor. |
| Total Spend Trend Chart (4) | A line chart shows the spending trend over time for internal labor and external labor. |
| Top 5 Headcount by Category Chart (5) | A bar chart shows the top five headcount categories for internal labor and external labor. |
| Headcount Trend Chart (5) | A line chart shows the headcount trend over time for internal labor and external labor. |
| Labor Analysis Table (6) | This table includes columns such as role type, total spend year to date, total budget year to date, budget variance year to date, total IT headcount, planned headcount, and headcount variance. |
| Dimension Selector (7) | Use this selector to view the analysis by different dimensions, such as role type, location, or employee type. |
| Aggregate Totals Row (8) | The bottom row shows totals for the metrics in the labor analysis table. |

## Questions Answered

- How much have we spent on labor so far, and are we within budget?
- Which roles or areas are over or under budget?
- What share of our IT spend is going toward labor?
- How is spending split between employees and contractors?
- What is our current headcount, and how is it divided between internal and external
  resources?
- How does our actual headcount compare with what we planned?
- Which roles have the highest number of resources or costs?
- Are we overstaffed or understaffed anywhere?
- How is labor spending and headcount changing over time?
- Where do we need to optimize costs or adjust hiring plans?

## Recommended Actions

- Review roles that are over budget and identify the reasons for higher spending.
- Analyze headcount gaps to determine if additional resources are needed or if there is excess
  capacity.
- Evaluate roles with a high number of contractors and consider opportunities to convert them to
  full-time employees.
- Review the report regularly to stay informed about changes in spending and headcount
  trends.
- Drill down into detailed spend data to understand where costs are coming from.
- Compare current data with historical trends to identify any unusual patterns or changes.
- Track key issues and follow up on action items to ensure progress.
