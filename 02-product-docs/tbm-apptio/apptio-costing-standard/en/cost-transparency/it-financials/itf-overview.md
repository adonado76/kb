# IT Financials Overview

## Overview

IT Financials provides consolidated visibility into technology spend across operating
expenses (OpEx) and capital expenditures (CapEx). It brings together financial data from
cost centers, and accounts groups to support analysis of trends, variances, and cost
drivers. The capability helps organizations understand how technology funds are used,
identify opportunities for optimization, and communicate spend clearly to stakeholders.

IT Financials also standardizes how financial data is reviewed, enabling consistent
evaluation of actuals, budget, and forecast across all technology domains.

## Personas

- **IT Finance** - Reviews spend, monitors budget performance, and analyzes cost drivers.
- **Cost Center Owner**- Evaluates spend associated with specific cost centers and
  identifies variances.

## Use Cases

**Supported out-of-the-box**:

- **Spend Visibility** - Consolidate and review OpEx and CapEx across cost centers
  and accounts groups to identify major cost drivers and understand budget variances.
- **Budget Variance Analysis** - Compare actual spend with budget and forecast to
  identify deviations, understand month-to-month trends, and support more accurate budget
  planning.
- **Transaction-Level Insights**- Drill into general-ledger transactions to identify
  variance drivers, validate spend classification, and strengthen cost controls.
- **Spend Reduction Analysis** - Identify fixed and variable spend to uncover
  opportunities to reduce run-rate costs and improve the overall cost structure.
- **Cost Pool Analysis** - Analyze OpEx distribution by Cost Pools (standard
  Technology Business Management (TBM) and Apptio TBM Unified Model (ATUM) categories that
  group IT spend into logical functional areas and benchmark IT spend against revenue and
  per-user metrics to identify potential areas for improvement.

**Custom use cases**

- **Accruals Management** - Manage accruals prior to month-end close to reduce
  inaccuracies and avoid unexpected budget variances.

## Outcomes

- Consolidated visibility into IT operating and capital spend.
- Clear understanding of cost trends, variances, and cost drivers.
- Accurate comparison of actuals, budget, and forecast.
- Improved cost control through detailed transaction-level insight.
- Better-informed budget planning and forecasting.
- Transparent communication of technology costs through show back.
- Improved ability to trace cost flow across infrastructure, applications, and services.
- Enhanced support for investment decisions using TCO insights.
