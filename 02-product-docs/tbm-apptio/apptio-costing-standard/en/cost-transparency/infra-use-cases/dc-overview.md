# Data Centers Overview

## Overview

Organizations are facing increasing pressure to manage growing data center demand driven by AI
workloads, hybrid IT architectures, cloud repatriation, and increasing rack densities. At the same
time, rising power, cooling, and operational costs are making it more difficult to understand the
true economics of operating data centers across regions and facilities. Limited visibility into
capacity constraints, utilization, facility operations, and allocation of data center costs to
infrastructure and business demand creates challenges for consolidation planning, investment
prioritization, and long-term capacity management.

IBM Apptio Data Center TCO enables organizations to gain enterprise-wide visibility into data
center facility operations, unit economics, and capacity constraints across owned, leased, and
colocation sites. The solution combines financial, operational, and capacity data to provide
transparency into power, cooling, space utilization, and cost drivers while supporting showback and
chargeback to infrastructure, applications, and business services. This helps organizations make
informed decisions around optimization, expansion, migration, consolidation, and sustainability
initiatives.

## Personas

- **IT Finance**
    
  Analyzes data center costs, tracks operational and capital spending, and
  evaluates unit economics across sites and regions.
- **CIO / CTO and IT Leadership**   
  Oversee data center strategy,
  investment planning, modernization, and long-term infrastructure decisions.
- **Data Center and Facility Owners**   
  Manage facility operations,
  monitor power, cooling, and space utilization, and plan for future capacity requirements.
- **Infrastructure and Capacity Managers**  
  Track utilization, identify
  constrained or underutilized sites, and support infrastructure optimization and allocation
  decisions.
- **Application and Service Owners**  
  Understand how applications and services
  consume data center resources to support showback, migration, and optimization planning.

## Use Cases

- **Data Center TCO & Unit Economics**   
  Understand total data center
  cost, capacity, and power consumption across sites and facility types while analyzing unit economics
  such as cost per rack, cost per area, and cost per kilowatt.
- **Capacity Utilization & Constraint Planning**   
  Identify available
  versus committed capacity across power, cooling, and space to proactively manage constraints,
  utilization, and future expansion planning.
- **Vendor, Labor & Contract Spend Transparency**   
  Analyze facility,
  labor, vendor, and contract-related costs to improve procurement decisions, contract management, and
  operational efficiency.
- **Infrastructure Cost Allocation**  
  Allocate shared data center facility
  costs to compute and storage infrastructure to improve cost transparency and establish defensible
  infrastructure unit costs.
- **Application & Service Cost Allocation**  
  Allocate data center costs to
  applications, products, and services to support showback, chargeback, and business-aligned
  investment decisions.

## Outcomes

- Improved visibility into enterprise-wide data center costs, utilization, and operational
  efficiency across regions and facilities.
- Better understanding of unit economics, including cost per rack, cost per area, and cost per
  kilowatt, to support investment and optimization decisions.
- More proactive management of power, cooling, and space constraints through capacity and
  headroom analysis.
- Enhanced ability to support data center consolidation, migration, expansion, and
  sustainability initiatives with defensible financial insights.
- Stronger alignment between facility operations, infrastructure consumption, and business
  demand through showback and chargeback transparency.
- Reduced operational inefficiencies and improved long-term planning through integrated financial
  and operational analytics.
