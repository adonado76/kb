# Adoption and communication patterns

If you do not intentionally socialize how to use Benchmarking, people will either ignore it or weaponize it.

## Standard communication assets

- One-page Benchmarking overview What it is Who owns it When we use it (planning, QBRs, Resource Tower reviews)
- A couple of real examples Case where a benchmark helped defend spend Case where a benchmark highlighted a realistic optimization
- Mini FAQ (exec-facing) What is our peer group? How precise are these metrics? Does above median always mean bad?

## Training for executives vs analysts

- Focus on reading charts and understanding peer groups and limitations
- Emphasize how benchmarks guide questions , not dictate answers
- Avoid TBM configuration details

- Train on drill paths, data lineage, and mapping decisions
- Give them scripts for common objections
- Walk through Troubleshooting & FAQ scenarios: blank charts, weird values, peer disputes
