# Benchmark types

- Industry benchmarks What they cover Enterprise-level spend and labor metrics by industry High-level comparisons such as IT spend as a percentage of revenue, IT labor mix, and other macro indicators What they are useful for Understanding your overall IT investment profile relative to peers in your industry Supporting high-level strategy and board-level conversations Sanity-checking whether your total IT spend is broadly in line for your size and sector
- IT OpEx benchmarks What they cover Operating expense distributions by Cost Pool and Resource Tower Archetype profiles that represent common operating models (for example vendor-centric, people-centric) What they are useful for Validating your spend structure at both financial and infrastructure layers Comparing your mix of labor, hardware, software, outside services, and facilities to peers Checking whether your spend profile matches your intended operating model
- Infrastructure benchmarks What they cover Unit costs and FTE efficiency metrics by Resource Tower and Sub-Tower Examples: cost per server, cost per TB of storage, cost per network port, FTEs per device What they are useful for Evaluating cost and efficiency of specific infrastructure domains Understanding where towers or sub-towers are materially above or below peer unit costs Supporting tower-level optimization and sourcing decisions

In practice, many customers start with industry metrics to frame the big picture, move to IT OpEx benchmarks to look at spend structure, then dive into infrastructure benchmarks for detailed analysis and initiative scoping.
