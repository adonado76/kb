# Frequently asked questions about methodology

A few stock answers you can adapt.

## Q. How precise are these benchmarks?

They are consistent and statistically grounded, not precise to the penny. Treat them as a way to see whether you are low, typical, or high compared to peers, then confirm with your own data before making big decisions.

## Q. Can we see who our peers are?

No. Peer data is anonymized. You see the distribution and statistics, not named organizations.

## Q. Do you remove outliers?

Most datasets apply sensible outlier handling and always require minimum peer counts. Extremely unusual values may be trimmed from the distribution or cause a metric to be suppressed for that peer group.

## Q. Why does a metric disappear when I change filters?

Because the filtered peer group falls below the minimum sample size or too few peers reported that metric under those filters. Widening the peer group usually brings it back.

## Q. Why does this metric not match what Finance reports?

Finance may use a different definition or scope. Benchmark metrics use standard TBM definitions so they can be compared across organizations. Where there is a mismatch, reconcile scope and definitions first, then decide which view to use for which purpose.

## Q. Can we change how a benchmark metric is calculated?

No. Benchmark definitions are standardized so that everyone is comparing the same thing. You can create custom internal metrics in Costing, but those are separate from the external benchmarks.
