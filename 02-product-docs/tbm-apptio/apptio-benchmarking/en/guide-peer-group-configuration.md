# Peer group configuration

![#: Interactive Benchmarking peer group options](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/peer-group-options.png)

1. Go to the Industry Benchmarks report collection
1. Click on the “Add Industry Peer” button and select up to three options.

![steps to select up to three peer groups](../../resources/images/it%20benchmarking_images/steps-peer-groups.png)

## Industry Peer Groups for Industry Metrics

Industry Peer Groups are standardized industry categories used to define the peer benchmarks you compare against. The table below provides a description for each peer group.

| Industry Peer Group | Description |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Automotive | Companies that design, build, sell, finance, service, or supply motor vehicles, including OEMs, dealerships, parts suppliers, and after-sales networks. |
| Banking & Finance | Financial institutions and services firms providing consumer and business banking, payments, lending, investing, brokerage, advisory, clearing, audit, and related financial operations. |
| Biotech, Pharma & Medical Equipment | Life sciences organizations developing or producing therapeutics, vaccines, diagnostics, and biotech solutions, plus manufacturers of medical, dental, lab, and clinical equipment and devices. |
| Central & Government Banks | Central banks and government-affiliated monetary authorities focused on currency issuance, reserves, monetary policy, public finance, and financial system stability. |
| Chemicals | Producers of industrial and specialty chemicals, including fertilizers, polymers, coatings, industrial gases, synthetics, and chemical inputs used across manufacturing sectors. |
| Commercial Banking | Traditional commercial banks primarily focused on deposit taking, business and retail lending, treasury services, and core banking operations. |
| Construction & Engineering | Firms delivering construction and engineering services for residential, commercial, industrial, and infrastructure projects, including specialty trades and construction materials. |
| Consumer Products | Manufacturers and brands of consumer-facing goods such as electronics, home and personal products, appliances, furnishings, toys, sporting goods, apparel, and luxury items. |
| Defense Contractors | Organizations whose primary business is delivering products and services to defense and national security customers, including aerospace, systems, logistics, and defense-focused technology. |
| Distribution, Transportation & Logistics | Companies moving, storing, and distributing goods and people, including wholesalers, freight and parcel networks, shipping, rail, airlines, trucking, ports, and warehousing. |
| Education | K to 12 and vocational education providers, plus education services such as tutoring, test prep, childcare, and online learning platforms. |

| Information Technology | Companies that build or deliver software, internet services, cloud platforms, managed services, and technology outsourcing. |

| Oil & Gas | Organizations involved in exploration, drilling, production, refining, distribution, storage, and transportation of petroleum and natural gas, including pipelines and field services. |
| Professional Services | Firms delivering specialized services such as consulting, legal, accounting, design, marketing, engineering services, HR services, research, and managed professional services. |
| Property & Casualty Insurance | Insurance carriers primarily focused on property and casualty lines, including personal and commercial coverage (auto, home, liability, specialty). |

## Select a default peer group

- Same or similar industry.
- Same or similar revenue band.
- Same region or global view that matches your IT scope.

- Click on a bar in the Region’s bar chart to set your default by specific region.
- Click on a bar in the Revenue’s bar chart to set your default by revenue range.
- Click on a box in the Industry-specific Distribution box plot to set your default by global industry.
