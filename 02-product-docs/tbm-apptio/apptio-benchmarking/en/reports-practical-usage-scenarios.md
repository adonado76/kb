# Practical usage scenarios

Here are a few high value ways to use the reports without overcomplicating things.

Defending a budget that looks “too high” The situation…Finance or the board thinks IT spend is high and wants cuts.

1. Start with an IT Spend view: IT spend as a percentage of Resource Tower spend Cost Pool compared to benchmarks Show where you sit relative to your Archetype.
1. Move to Application Distribution : IT spend percentage broken down to Resource Sub-Towers within the “Application” Resource Tower and compared to benchmarks Show where you sit relative to your Archetype.
1. Only then, if needed, go into Infrastructure and benchmark-enabled Costing for specific towers.

Targeting a cost optimization initiative The situation…you need to find credible savings without random across-the-board cuts.

1. Use IT OpEx and Infrastructure views to find outlier towers or cost pools.
1. For a chosen outlier, open a benchmark-enabled Costing report.
1. Drill to identify drivers: Specific vendors Specific regions or data centers Specific services or platforms.
1. Turn these into initiatives: License optimization Rationalization and consolidation Sourcing changes Hardware refresh or tiering.

Planning tower transformation The situation…you plan a multi-year change for a tower such as End User, Network, or Storage.

1. Use Infrastructure benchmarks to baseline current unit cost and FTE efficiency.
1. Define a realistic target band based on quartiles, not the absolute minimum.
1. Work with the tower owner to identify: What architecture, tooling, or sourcing changes would move you toward that band, What investment is required
1. Track progress annually using the same benchmark metrics.
