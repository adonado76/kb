# Integration with the broader TBM program

Benchmarking is one component, not the whole program.

- Costing Benchmark gaps guide where to invest time refining the cost model Costing drill-downs explain why benchmarks look the way they do
- Planning & budgeting Benchmarks provide an external reference for targets and constraints Use them as guardrails for Opex, capex balance, and tower budgets
- Portfolio / product or service management Benchmarks help challenge whether certain services, platforms, or products are over- or under-invested vs peers
- Risk and resilience Being “cheap” compared to peers is not always good if it implies underinvestment in resilience or security Use benchmarks to balance cost and risk, not just to chase the lowest number

If your TBM story is, “We have Costing, Planning, and Benchmarking working together to shape decisions.” you are in the right place. If Benchmarking is just “that extra thing with pretty charts we pull out once a year,” this section is your blueprint for fixing that.
