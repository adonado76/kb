# Core workflows

Here is where Benchmarking shows up in real work.

New program rollout When: Benchmarking is newly implemented, or a new business unit is brought into scope.

1. Benchmarking Owner validates core configuration and basic views. NOTE: See Quickstart & Configuration Guide sections for more information.
1. TBM Lead defines the approved default peer group for that unit.
1. Benchmarking Owner prepares a concise initial pack of 3–5 views: 1 Industry view (IT spend vs revenue) 1 OpEx structure view 1–2 infra views if available
1. TBM Lead walks through with sponsor (CIO / VP-level): Here is where we stand vs peers Here are the gaps that merit further investigation
1. Capture decisions: Which metrics will be tracked regularly Which towers / areas are priority How often you will revisit benchmarks

## Annual IT planning

1. By end of 2 nd quarter of a calendar year, Benchmarking Owner refreshes Benchmarking to the latest full year and validates core metrics.
1. For each major Resource Tower or domain: Review unit costs and OpEx structures vs peers Identify where you are significantly high or low
1. TBM Lead and Finance use this to: Inform efficiency targets (for example “move End User support toward the 60th percentile over 3 years”) Sanity-check large investment or reduction proposals against relevant benchmarks

Output: targets and planning narratives anchored in external context, not just internal history.

## Resource Tower optimization initiative

When: You choose a Resource Tower or function for targeted improvement.

1. Use infra and OpEx benchmarks to size the gap: Unit cost variance Spend share vs peers
1. Use benchmark-enabled Costing reports to drill into: Cost pools Vendors and contracts Regions and data centers Services or applications
1. Resource Tower Owner and TBM Lead translate gaps into levers: Sourcing or renegotiation Consolidation / retirement Automation and tooling Architecture standardization
1. Track progress annually using the same benchmark metrics and peer definitions.

## Executive challenge session

When: A senior stakeholder does not trust or like what the benchmarks show.

1. Start with definition and peer group , not the gap: “We are comparing ourselves to [industry, region, size band], year [N].” “This metric is defined as [numerator / denominator].”
1. Show your position vs quartiles.
1. Immediately follow with internal drill: Use Costing to show the underlying costs and volumes. Clarify inclusions and exclusions.
1. If there is genuine scope or mapping disagreement: Log it as an issue. Agree either to adjust configuration or to document the rationale and keep it.
