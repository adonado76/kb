# Peer group and filter problems

Metrics disappear when filters are applied

- Peer group falls below the minimum required sample size for that metric
- Too few organizations in that slice report the metric
- Dataset is suppressing results to maintain participant anonymity

1. Remove filters one at a time and see when the metric comes back.
1. Note which filter (industry subsegment, region, size band) is causing thin samples.
1. Use a slightly broader peer group for any metric that regularly disappears.

Stakeholder disputes peer group choice

- Industry classification doesn’t match how they see the business
- Size band or region feels off to them
- They are reacting to an uncomfortable gap, not a genuinely bad peer group

1. Confirm how your organization is classified in profile (industry, size, region) and why.
1. Test a small number of alternate peer groups and see how much results move.
1. Check that their requested group isn’t cherry-picking (for example small subset that makes you look good).

Short answer you can send We set the peer group to match our primary industry, size band, and region. We can test alternate groups, but we need to pick one definition and stick with it for decisions, not change peers every time a number is uncomfortable.
