# Edition and Costing notices

*Billing always relies on a Costing project, but this guide keeps the labeling simple by focusing on Billing editions:*

- Billing Essentials runs within a Costing Essentials project.
- Billing Standard runs as a separate endpoint in addition to a Costing Standard project.

Whenever a topic applies to only one Billing edition, a notice appears at the top of the section or subsection:

- Notice: Applies to Billing Essentials only.
- Notice: Applies to Billing Standard only.

If a section does not show one of these notices at the top, it applies to both Billing Essentials and Billing Standard.

You can assume the following:

- Any content marked Billing Essentials implicitly includes the Costing Essentials project it runs on.
- Any content marked Billing Standard implicitly includes the Costing Standard project and endpoint it runs on.

Within a section, specific steps or tables may also be called out as “Billing Essentials only” or “Billing Standard only” where behavior or configuration diverges.

## Conventions used in this guide

To keep things consistent:

- “Costing” means the IBM Apptio product formerly known as Cost Transparency.
- “IBM Apptio Billing” and “Billing” mean the overall billing product (including all editions).
- “TBM Studio” means the configuration and modeling environment used to build and maintain Costing and Billing content. Tasks such as loading data, defining models, designing reports, and managing builds and environments are performed in TBM Studio by users with appropriate access.
- “TBM” means the Technology Business Management discipline and standard taxonomy for classifying Technology spend.
