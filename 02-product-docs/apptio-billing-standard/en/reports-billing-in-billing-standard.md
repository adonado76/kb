# Billing reports in Billing Standard

*Billing Standard includes domain-specific views, invoices, and summary reports. Reports are grouped into sections shown in the home report’s page. Unless noted, reports are installed via the “BoIT- Foundation” component.*

## Business Owner

- Purpose: Provide a single, executive-friendly view of what’s being consumed and charged back, how that usage is changing over time, and what is driving the change across all business units, including service trends, unit activity, charge concentration by a selectable dimension (example shown: location), and budget variance.
- Questions answered: What is the total charge for the current period, and how did it change month over month? What is the change vs the previous period? What is the current period, quarter, year-to-date (YTD) charge? What is the charge per Department, Business Unit, Location, and Cost Center, and is it trending up or down? Which services are growing the fastest (MoM %) and likely driving spend increases? How are charges trending over the last 13 periods by service type (Delivery, Shared, End User, etc.)? What is the budget variance for the current month, quarter or YTD, overall and by service type? Which service types are the largest contributors to over/under budget (treemap and variance details)?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Usage Summary report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a billing period invoice view that shows what was charged vs budget, how charges are trending (month and quarter), and, most importantly, a driver-based explanation of why charges changed (new or stopped services, price changes, rate changes, consumption changes, and allocation changes).
- Questions answered: What is the total charge for the current month, and is it up or down month over month? What was the budget for the month, and are we over or under budget? What are the current quarter charges, and how do they compare quarter over quarter? What is the budget quarter-to-date (QTD), and what is the QTD variance? How are annualized charge, charge YTD, and annual budget trending across the fiscal year (variance trend)? Did any services start being charged this month or stop being charged this month (added and removed services)? What charge impact came from effective price changes? What charge impact came from unit rate changes (rate changes using last month’s units)? What charge impact came from consumption changes (unit changes using last month’s effective price)? What charge impact came from allocation changes for ongoing service offerings? Which specific service offerings contributed the most to the increase or decrease, and by how much? If someone asks “why did my bill change?”, what are the top driver categories and their dollar impacts?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Invoice report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provides a high-level invoice summary for the selected period and scope (example shown: All Business Units) that highlights current charges, quarter-to-date and year-to-date totals, shows where the bill is concentrated (top services and service types over time), and gives a table-based breakdown by service including units, effective price, planned units, charge change, and budget variance, with an easy jump to the Detailed Invoice when deeper explanation is needed.
- Questions answered: What is the total charge for the current period? What are the charge QTD and charge YTD totals? What are the top services by current month charge, and how much are they contributing? How are charges trending over recent months by service type (Delivery, Shared, End User, etc.)? Within a service type, which service categories and services make up the spend? For a given service, what are the units, unit of measure, and effective price driving the charge? Are planned units present, and how do actual units compare (when populated)? Which services have the biggest up/down change vs the prior period (trend indicator in the table)? What is the budget, budget variance, and variance % by service (when budget is populated)? Which services are the biggest contributors to over/under budget? Where should I drill next to explain “why did this change?” (via See Detailed Invoice)?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Invoice Overview report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide an interactive, ad hoc invoice workbench that lets users pivot, filter, and slice charges across services and business-unit hierarchies, and view key billing metrics (charge, units, effective price, annualized charge, service offering count, and prior-period deltas) for current month, quarter, YTD, and annual views. It’s the report you use to answer “what exactly am I paying for, where is it coming from, and what changed?” with precision, without needing to leave the invoice context.
- Questions answered: What are my charges for the selected scope (all business units, a BU, cost center, etc.) for current month / quarter / YTD / annual? Which service types, categories, services, or service offerings make up the bill, and how much does each contribute? For any slice, what are the units, unit of measure, and effective price associated with the charge? What is the annualized charge for a service or group, based on current period run-rate? How many service offerings are contributing to the total? What changed vs the prior period? Did units change vs the prior period? Which services or organizations are the largest drivers of the period-over-period change? How do charges break down by business attributes like Location, Lifecycle Stage, Business Process, Business Capability, or Business Criticality? How do charges differ when pivoting by Business Unit, Owner, Department, or Cost Center? If someone disputes a bill, can I isolate the exact slice of data that explains it (service + org + attribute + time period)?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Detailed Invoice report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a single place to monitor how actual charges are tracking against budget and forecast for the selected scope (example shown: All Business Units), show the trend across the fiscal year, and identify the services driving the largest overages or underages for the current month, current quarter, or year to date.
- Questions answered: What is the budget variance for the current period, and what is the variance YTD? What is the monthly budget and budget YTD, and how does it compare to actuals? What is the actual charge for the month, and what is the charge YTD? Over the current fiscal year, how are Charge, Business Budget, and Business Forecast trending month by month? Are we trending toward being over budget or under budget, and when did the gap start widening? Which services are the top drivers of variance (top services over current month budget)? For each service, what are the budget, charge, forecast, and resulting budget variance? Which services have the clearest upward/downward trend that might explain recurring variance? How do variances change when switching the time lens between Current Month, Current Quarter, and Year to Date? Where should I focus first to reduce variance, based on the largest variance contributors?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Budget Variance report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a consolidated view of application-level charges and usage so you can understand which applications are driving Billing charges, how those charges are trending period over period, and how spend breaks down by common app dimensions (service hierarchy, application type, lifecycle, hierarchy, business owner, business criticality). It’s essentially “the bill, but through an application lens”, including drillable detail by application name.
- Questions answered: What is the total application-related charge for the current month, and how did it change MoM? What are charge QTD and charge YTD for applications? How many applications are in scope (application count), and what is the application license count (if populated)? Which application domains or groupings are increasing or decreasing period over period (treemap change view)? What are the top applications by charge, and how are they trending over time (top 5 trend chart)? Which applications have the largest charge increases or decreases vs the prior period? For an individual application, what are the billable units, unit of measure, and charge? Which application business owners have the highest charges, and which owners’ charges are changing the most? How do charges differ by application type, lifecycle stage, business criticality, or application hierarchy? Which services (from the service hierarchy) are associated with each application and what are their charges? Where do we have potential candidates for review based on combinations like high cost + low criticality, or rising cost + end-of-life lifecycle?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Applications report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a cloud-focused view of Billing that compares raw cloud provider charges to the fully loaded charge to deliver cloud services, then breaks that spend down by service, provider/product line items, and key business dimensions (service hierarchy, location, business criticality, lifecycle). It’s built to help you see what cloud is costing, what you’re billing/showing back, and what’s driving changes over time.
- Questions answered: What is the current period cloud charge, and how is it trending over time? How much of cloud cost is provider charge versus full charge to deliver (the uplift from allocations, shared services, overhead, etc.)? Which cloud services are driving the most cost right now (charge breakdown by service)? How has the provider vs full charge gap changed over the last several periods? Which service types (Delivery, Shared, End User, etc.) contain the cloud charges, and what are their charges and changes? For each cloud service, what are the associated units, unit of measure, effective price, and charge change? Where are cloud charges concentrated when slicing by: Service Hierarchy Location Business Criticality Lifecycle Stage Which providers (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP, etc., depending on your data) are driving the charges? Which products / line items (compute, storage, database services, SaaS items, etc.) are driving the provider charges? Which provider items show the biggest units change or charge movement period over period?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Cloud report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a project-centric view of Billing so you can see which projects are driving charges, how project spend is trending over time, and how that spend breaks down by common project dimensions (service hierarchy, project type, lifecycle, business initiative, spend type). It’s basically “the bill, but through a project portfolio lens”, with enough detail to support portfolio reviews and charge conversations.
- Questions answered: What is the total project-related charge for the current month, and how did it change MoM? What are charge QTD and charge YTD for projects? How many projects are in scope (and how many are active, if populated)? How is overall project spend trending over the last several periods? Which projects have the highest charges in the current period? Which projects have the biggest period-over-period charge change (in dollars and percent)? What does the recent trend look like for each project (6 period trend sparkline)? How do charges roll up by project portfolio / program / business initiative? Which project managers are associated with the highest charges or fastest growth? How do project charges vary by project type, project lifecycle, or spend type? Which services (from the business service hierarchy) are tied to project charges, and what are those charges?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst

Fig #: Projects report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide the authoritative, filterable catalog of billable/showback services and service offerings, including their owners (service manager), pricing, unit of measure, and key classification attributes (service hierarchy, pricing methodology, business capability, business criticality, business process, descriptions, in service and retirement dates). This is the place people go to answer, “what services do we offer, who owns them, and how are they priced?”
- Questions answered: What services and service offerings exist in the Billing catalog? How are services organized in the Service Library Hierarchy (service type, category, etc.)? Who is the Service Manager (owner) for each service or offering? What is the price for each service offering? What is the unit of measure for each service offering (how it’s billed)? Which services use which pricing methodology (if populated)? Which services are mapped to a given business capability or business process? Which services are tagged with a given business criticality level? What is the service description (and other descriptive fields) for each service/offering? Which services are in service versus retired or scheduled for retirement (in service, retirement dates)? Are there services with missing or suspicious data (no owner, $0 price, missing UoM, missing descriptions), so we can fix the catalog?
- Target users: Consumer, Senior Leader, Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Service Library report in Billing Standard

## Business Relationship Manager

- Purpose: Provide a single, executive-style view that ties together what was charged, what it cost to deliver, and how much was recovered from consumers, then highlights where recovery is over or under by service. It’s the “are we recovering what we spend, and which services are the culprits?” dashboard, with trend, top movers, and drillable detail.
- Questions answered: What is the total Charge for the current period, and how did it change MoM? What is the Cost to deliver those services (current and YTD)? What is Recovery Variance (Charge minus Cost), current and YTD? Do we have a Business Budget for this scope, and what is the budget YTD (if populated)? Which services are the fastest growing MoM by percent? How are charges trending over the last 13 periods by service type? Which services are under-recovering the most (top services under, negative recovery variance)? Which services are over-recovering the most (top services over, positive recovery variance)? For a given service, what are the Charge, Cost, Recovery Variance, and Effective Price? For a given service, what are the billable units, unit of measure, and cost per unit (if populated)? Where are the biggest budget variances (current month / current quarter / YTD), and which service types drive them? Is the problem primarily pricing/recovery (charge vs cost gap) or primarily budget management (budget variance)?
- Target users: Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Business Relationship Summary report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a business-unit-focused view of recovery performance by comparing charges vs costs (and optionally budget/forecast) at a selectable org level (Business Unit, Owner, Department, Cost Center), highlighting where you are over- or under-recovering, and projecting true-up (credit/debit) expectations for the rest of the year based on year-to-date patterns. It’s the “who’s paying vs who’s costing, and what true-up is coming?” report.
- Questions answered: At the selected org level (BU/Owner/Department/Cost Center), where are we over-recovering or under-recovering this month (variance treemap)? For a given org slice, what are the charges vs costs (or charge YTD vs cost YTD)? Which orgs are the biggest contributors to overall recovery variance (positive or negative)? What does recovery variance look like YTD by selected org level (trend line over the year)? What is the % recovered for each org (how much cost is being recovered through charges)? How do results change when the primary metric is Charge vs Cost vs Charge YTD vs Cost YTD, and the comparison metric is Cost/Budget/Forecast (and their YTD versions)? Based on YTD behavior, what is the projected full-year charge and projected full-year cost by org? What is the expected true-up credit or debit by org (projected charge minus projected cost)? Which orgs have consistent monthly behavior (good candidates for the projection method) vs volatile ones that may need manual review? How do recovery results change when filtering by service hierarchy (for example, only Delivery Services or only Cloud Services)? Where should I drill next for deeper analysis (link to Detailed Cost Transparency View)?
- Target users: Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Recovery by Business report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide an interactive, ad hoc workbench to analyze recovery by letting users pivot and filter charges, costs, budgets, forecasts, units, and recovery variance across service hierarchy layers and business unit hierarchy levels, for multiple time lenses (current month, quarter, YTD, annual, and multi-period views). It’s the drill-anywhere report you use when someone asks, “are we recovering our costs, exactly where are we off, and what’s driving it?”
- Questions answered: For the selected scope, what are the charges vs costs, and what is the resulting recovery variance? Which services (by service type, category, name, offering) are the biggest drivers of under-recovery or over-recovery? At which org level (Business Unit, Owner, Department, Cost Center) are we under/over recovering, and by how much? What is the % recovered implied by charge vs cost for a given slice (charge divided by cost)? How do recovery results change when filtering by attributes like business criticality, business capability, business process, lifecycle stage, or unit of measure (when populated)? For any slice, what are the units and planned billable units, and are they trending in a way that explains recovery gaps? How do budget and forecast compare to actuals for the same slice (and what is the business budget variance)? How does recovery look across time: current month vs current quarter vs YTD, or across months (year) / quarters (year)? If a business unit disputes recovery or true-up, can I isolate the exact service + org + attribute + time combination that explains the variance?
- Target users: Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Detailed Recovery report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide an operational console to distribute invoices via email for the selected billing period, showing who will receive each invoice, the pre-built message that will be sent, and the send status. It’s basically the “push the button, prove it was sent, and resend if needed” part of the monthly billing process.
- Questions answered: Which invoices are queued to be emailed for this billing period (by business unit, department, location, etc.)? Who is each invoice being sent to (recipient email address)? What message will be included in the email for each invoice? Which invoices are Not Sent vs Sent (status tracking)? Can indicate operationally: what is still outstanding if I hide sent and focus on what remains. Can I quickly find a specific invoice distribution item (search)? Can I send all emails at once, or send selectively (via the action controls)? After sending, which recipients can I prove received a send attempt from the system (via status)?
- Additional guidance: Persons who can receive an invoice are entered into the Recipient Invoice List table. Consider maintaining the list in an Excel file and uploading as needed. To include a global message, header and footer for all invoices, add an entry into the following fields in the Email Invoice Messages table: Global Message (displayed at the top of every invoice in addition to any header) Default Header Message (displayed at the top of every invoice if the same-named field not defined in the Recipient Invoice List table) Default Footer Message (displayed at the top of every invoice if the same-named field not defined in the Recipient Invoice List table).
- Target users: Admin, Analyst

Fig #: Distribution report in Billing Standard

## Service Provider

- Purpose: Provide a service-provider view of Billing that summarizes charges, costs, budget, and recovery variance across the full service portfolio (the “IT as a provider” lens), highlights trending services and top movers, and surfaces where services are over- or under-recovering and over/under budget at the service, category, and offering levels.
- Questions answered: What is the total Charge for all services this period, and how did it change MoM? What is the total Cost to deliver all services (current and YTD)? What is total Recovery Variance (Charge minus Cost), current and YTD? What is the total Budget (and budget YTD), and are we trending over or under budget (if populated)? Which services are the fastest growing MoM by percent? How are charges trending over the last 13 periods by service type (Delivery, Shared, End User, etc.)? Which services are the biggest under-recovery drivers (top services under)? Which services are the biggest over-recovery drivers (top services over)? For each service in detail, what are the billable units, effective price, cost per unit, charge, cost, and recovery variance? Which service types/categories/offerings are driving charge-to-budget variance and cost-to-budget variance (treemap + table)? Is a recovery issue driven by cost (cost rising) or charge/pricing (charge not keeping up with cost)?
- Target users: Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: IT Service Provider Summary report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a focused view of recovery health for the service portfolio by showing how charges compare to costs (and optionally budget/forecast), how recovery variance is trending (current month vs YTD across the fiscal year), where recovery is concentrated by business unit, and how much of the bill is influenced by adjustments. It’s the dashboard for answering: “Are we recovering what we spend, where are we off, and is it getting better or worse?”
- Questions answered: What are the current totals for Charge, Cost, Budget, and Recovery Variance (and their YTD values)? Are we over-recovering or under-recovering overall, and by how much? How is recovery variance trending across the fiscal year, for current month and YTD? Which services are the fastest growing MoM by percent (early warning for recovery pressure)? Where is recovery concentrated by business unit (current month charge vs cost treemap)? Which business units drive the most cost and which drive the most recovery variance (table with % of cost)? At the service level, which service offerings have the highest cost, and how do budget/forecast compare (service recovery details table)? Which services have the largest adjustments, and how much of their total charge is adjustment-driven? If adjustments are driving recovery variance, which services should be reviewed first for pricing/adjustment policy? Where should I drill next for deeper cost tracing (link to Detailed Cost Transparency View)?
- Target users: Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Recovery Analysis report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide a unit-of-measure view of Billing that tracks cost per unit vs charge per unit over time, so you can evaluate unit economics, rate stability, and recovery performance for key consumption units (accounts, mailboxes, devices, desktops, etc.). It helps answer whether your unit rates are priced appropriately, drifting, or creating over/under recovery, and ties those unit rates back to budget, forecast, and planned units.
- Questions answered: For a selected unit of measure, how have cost per unit and charge per unit trended over time? Is the gap between charge per unit and cost per unit widening or shrinking (recovery pressure)? What is the running average unit cost, and is unit cost trending up or down? Which units of measure have the highest cost per unit or highest charge per unit right now? For each unit of measure, what are the billable units and how do they compare to planned billable units? What is the recovery variance per unit (per-unit over/under recovery) by unit of measure? What is the budget per unit and forecast per unit, and how do they compare to actual unit cost and charge? Which units are showing abnormal changes month to month that suggest a pricing update, allocation change, or data issue? Should a specific service offering priced per a unit (like “User Account” or “Mailbox”) have its rate adjusted based on sustained unit economics?
- Target users: Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Unit Rate Analysis report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Provide an application-centric recovery view that compares charge vs cost for applications (and their related services), highlights recovery variance trends over time, and surfaces the applications driving over- or under-recovery. It’s designed for “are we recovering what it costs to provide/operate this application, and what’s changing?” conversations, with filters for app attributes and owners and a drill link to a detailed cost view.
- Questions answered: What is the total application charge for the current period, and how did it change MoM? What are charge QTD and charge YTD for applications? What is the budget variance (current and YTD) for application charges (when budget is populated)? Which applications are the biggest contributors to monthly recovery variance (top 5 + other over/under trend chart)? Is application recovery getting better or worse over time, and which apps drive the trend? For a specific application (or app slice), what are the billable units and unit of measure supporting the charge? For each application, what are the cost, cost change, charge, charge change, and resulting recovery variance? Which applications show rising cost without matching charge, indicating emerging under-recovery? How does recovery vary by application business owner (who owns the recovery problem)? How do results change when filtering by application type, lifecycle, hierarchy, or business criticality? Which services are tied to each application, and how do they influence the application’s recovery outcome? Where should I drill next to validate allocations and cost drivers (link to Detailed Cost Transparency View)?
- Target users: Service or Product Owner, Stakeholder, Senior Leader, Analyst, Admin

Fig #: Applications Recovery report in Billing Standard

How do cloud charges break down by a selected dimension (for example location, provider, product, or instance type)? How do charges for the selected breakdown trend over time (charge comparison over time)? Which specific combinations of location + product + provider + type + item are driving charges? Are there unexpected “new” or “spiky” cloud cost drivers when you change filters (provider/product/instance type)? How do cloud charges change when filtering to a specific cloud service hierarchy grouping? Which cloud products or services appear to have no consumption/charges (data quality signal when charts show zeros)? What is the detailed description of the provider line items behind the summarized charge views?

- Purpose: Provide visibility into modified charges created by transfer pricing rules and cross-charging so Finance and Technology leaders can understand how much of the bill is being adjusted for legal-entity and location-based accounting needs (for example, tax, intercompany settlement, and internal cross-charges), and how those adjustments trend over time.

For a given service name, what portion of its charges are being modified by cross-charge logic?

Fig #: Data Freshness report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Helps admins validate that the consumption records driving billable units and charges are structurally sound before invoices are published or distributed, specifically by monitoring feed volume stability, unit-of-measure consistency, and whether any key fields are missing or not published (which can lead to wrong rates, mixed units, or confusing invoices).
- Questions answered: Did the consumption feed load the expected amount of data this month? (row-count trend over the last 13 periods) Are there spikes or drop-offs in consumption volume that suggest a missing file, duplicate load, or mapping break? Are billable units being reported in the correct Unit of Measure for each service? Where do the “Business Services” Unit of Measure and the “Consumption Feed” Unit of Measure disagree? Which services and service offerings have unit-of-measure mismatches that could produce incorrect effective rates or charges? Which records are failing validation right now (status flags), and how big is the impact (charges, billable units)? Are there any unpublished or missing fields that will prevent expected invoice detail from showing up? Is the percentage of unpublished fields trending up (a slow-motion quality degradation)? Which specific service IDs/records are affected so I can route fixes to the right owner (service manager, data owner)?
- Target users: Admin, Analyst

Fig #: Consumption Feed Quality report in Billing Standard

- Purpose: Checks that the service hierarchy is structurally valid and that required service fields are populated, so Billing Standard can calculate prices and units consistently, roll up services correctly, and produce clean, defensible invoices (no orphan services, no broken relationships, no missing required metadata).
- Questions answered: Do all services have valid 1:1 relationships across the service hierarchy layers (Service ID, Service Name, Service Offering), or are there duplicates/orphans? Which Service IDs have relationship errors, and how many errors exist per Service ID? Are any required fields blank for any service records? How many blanks exist by required field (Service ID, Unit of Measure, Service Type, Service Category, Service Offering, Service Name, Description)? Which specific service records have missing required fields, so I can fix them (and who owns them)? Are there services that will fail publishing or cause billing inaccuracies because key identifiers or UoM are missing? Do we have inconsistent service definitions that could fragment reporting, like the same Service Name tied to multiple offerings improperly (or vice versa)? Are we trending toward “death by a thousand cuts” in service hygiene (increasing error counts month to month, if trended elsewhere or via repeated reviews)?

- Questions answered: How much of my data is allocated vs unallocated (and is that improving or getting worse over time)? Which object is leaking value (highest unassigned charge, cost, budget, forecast, or units)? What is the % assigned by object, and which objects are below an acceptable threshold? Did unassigned amounts change vs last period, and by how much? Which specific configured allocations are driving the model, and what are they doing (source object, target object, method, filters, strategy, value)? Are any allocations behaving like “Do Not Allocate Up” traps, leaving charges stranded due to missing reference mappings? Where should I start troubleshooting, based on the object that has the most unassigned value? Which services have the biggest month over month charge variance (top absolute % variance)? Is a big variance driven by a real change or a modeling/config issue, based on the MoM detail table (current vs previous charge, absolute variance)? Are the biggest variances concentrated in a specific service type/category/offering, suggesting a systemic issue (like an input feed change or an allocation break)?
