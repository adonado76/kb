# Hypercare: first 1–3 cycles

*Go-live is not finished when the first period closes. The first few cycles are where weaknesses show up.*

## Operational hypercare

For the first one to three billing cycles:

- Increase monitoring and QA: Run exception and QA reports early and often. Track recurring issues (for example, common missing mappings, late feeds).
- Shorten feedback loops: Encourage Service or Product Owners and Finance to raise questions quickly. Allocate time for Admins and Analysts to investigate and fix issues within the same period.
- Stabilize processes: Adjust the runbook based on what actually happens (for example, data arrives later than expected). Break “hero behavior” habits and document the real process.

## Communication during hypercare

Basic expectations to set:

- There may be minor corrections in the first few periods.
- The overall structure of the bill will remain stable; only defects get fixed.
- Stakeholders should not rely on screenshots from pre-go-live prototypes; they should reference the live reports.

Keep communication concrete: “This month we fixed X and Y, next month you should see Z behavior.”
