# What each section covers

*Use this as a quick map of the guide:*

- Overview and Key Concepts - Definitions and core ideas used throughout Billing.
- User Roles and Permissions - Which roles exist, what they typically do, and how access is granted.
- Editions and Capabilities - Differences between Billing Essentials and Billing Standard, and when each is appropriate.
- Solution Architecture and Dependencies - How Billing is packaged as components, how it relies on Costing, and where endpoints fit in.
- Environments and Release Management - How environment builds affect Billing configuration and reports.
- Data Requirements and Integration - Required datasets, master tables, and common integration points for Billing to “light up.”
- Running the Billing Cycle - Annual and monthly activities to set rates, validate charges, and publish bills or journals.
- Working with Billing Reports - How to find, interpret, and export the key reports used by IT, Finance, and business units.
- Configuring Billing Essentials (Implementation) - Out-of-box setup steps specific to Billing Essentials.
- Configuring Billing Standard (Implementation) - Out-of-box setup steps specific to Billing Standard and its additional components.
- Advanced Topics - Transfer pricing, cloud reporting, project views, forecasting, and unit-rate optimization.
- Administration & Operations - Ongoing monitoring, permission changes, and operational housekeeping.
- Design Patterns & Use Cases - Sample patterns for common billing scenarios and how to implement them with Billing.
- Go-Live Playbook - Checklists and practices for moving Billing into Production and stabilizing the first cycles.
- Role-Based Training Guide - How different roles can build capability with Billing and which sections to focus on.
- Glossary & Acronyms - What each component adds (datasets, reports, metrics, and key dependencies), definitions for Billing and Costing terminology and common abbreviations.
- Troubleshooting and FAQ - Common questions, typical symptoms, and where to look next in the guide
