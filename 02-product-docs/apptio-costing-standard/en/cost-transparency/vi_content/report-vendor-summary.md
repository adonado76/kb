# Vendor Summary

◆ Applies to: Vendor Insights on TBM Studio 12.8 and later (v107)

Use the  **Vendor Summary**  report to analyze the total vendor portfolio
spending across vendor type and time. This report is designed for:

- CIO and senior IT leadership
- Application Owners
- Services Owners
- IT Finance Managers
- Vendor Managers

**Display the Vendor Summary report**

In the  Application  menu, select  Vendor Insights  .

1. Navigate to  Report Collections > Vendors  .
2. From the bar at the top of the page, select  Vendor Summary  .
3. To export or email your data, select  Export  ( ![img](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/vi_images/vi/export.jpg) ) at the top right of
   the page and select an export format.

The  **Vendor Summary**  reports contain the following elements:

![img](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/vi_images/vi/vi-vendorsummary.jpg)

**(1) Report collection**

This report collection provides the details you need to analyze your vendor portfolio spending
across vendor type and time:

- Vendor Summary (described in this article)

**(2) KPIs**

KPIs provide a high-level view of your vendor spend and contract expiration:

- **Vendor Spend**  - This KPI shows the total spend for the current period and
  the number of vendors spending in the current period. Spend is the sum of all account payables in
  the current period.
- **Vendor Spend YTD**  - This KPI shows the total vendor spend YTD and the
  difference in the YTD spend between the previous and current year.
- **Contracts Expiring < 90 Days**  - This KPI shows the number of contracts
  expiring in less than 90 days, and in less than 180 days.
- **Min Committed Spend for Contract Expiring < 90 Days**  - This KPI shows the
  minimum committed spend for contracts expiring in less than 90 days, and in less than 180 days.

**(3) Vendor Spend by Type**

Use this section to understand whether the vendor spend aligns with the vendor strategy as
categorized in the vendor type. For example, given that the vendors are categorized into Strategic,
Transactional, and Preferred vendor types, if the company strategy is to spend more with fewer
strategic vendors, this section will show the trending progress of that strategy based on actual
spend.

- Use the  **Year to Date**  chart to see vendor spend YTD as a percentage of
  total spend by vendor type. View the vendor spend for the vendor type by hovering over the vendor
  type.
- Use the  **Trend over Time**  chart to see the trend of monthly spend by
  vendor type over the past 12 months.

**(4) Vendor Spend across Time**

Use this section to understand the spending of specific vendors. Use the bar chart to see the
vendors with the highest spend. Use the table to view the details of those top vendors during the
selected time period, sorted by the highest spending vendors on top.

Click the tabs to view spend for the current period, current quarter, and YTD. The slicers for
vendor owner, vendor manager, and vendor type allow you to narrow the results as needed.

For additional details about a specific vendor, click in the chart or click on the **Parent Vendor Name**  column in the table to open the  [Vendor Detail report](report-vendor-detail.html)  .

**(5) Vendor Details**

Use this table to further analyze the vendor portfolio and vendor spend. You can filter the
table by various vendor attributes and see their spend in the current period, current quarter, and
YTD.

Use the options above the table to add specific details to your analysis, including IT tower,
parent vendor, vendor service, sub-tower, function, service, type of dependency, and location.

For additional details about a specific vendor, click in the  **Vendor Name** column to open the  [Vendor Detail report](report-vendor-detail.html) 
.

Questions answered

Use the information presented in this report to answer the
following questions:

- Which vendor types compose my vendor costs?
- How are we progressing against the vendor strategy? Does the spend reflect the vendor strategy?
- What changes should we make to re-balance vendor spend?
- How fragmented/concentrated is spending across vendors?
- Do we have redundant vendors?
- Where do we have variances in spend?
