# Hybrid IT TCO Impact Overview

Hybrid IT has become the standard operating model as organizations adopt a mix of
on-premises, private cloud, and public cloud platforms. While this approach increases
flexibility and scalability, it also introduces financial complexity. IT leaders are under
pressure to understand, track, and clearly demonstrate the financial impact of ongoing
application migrations and hybrid footprint changes.

The Hybrid IT TCO Impact capability enables organizations to quantify the true financial
impact of application migrations by providing visibility into Total Cost of Ownership (TCO),
unit costs, and consumption volumes across the evolving hybrid landscape. It helps leaders
validate migration business cases, track outcomes over time, and make informed decisions about
future migration strategies.

## Personas

• **IT Finance**

Evaluates the financial impact of application migrations and validates expected cost
outcomes.

• **Solution Owners (Application Owners)**

Assesses pre- and post-migration cost efficiency and identifies optimization
opportunities.

• **C-Suite Executives and IT Leadership**

Uses hybrid IT cost insights to justify migration investments and guide future
strategy.

## Use Cases

**Supported out-of-the-box:**

• **Hybrid IT: Compare
Current vs. Target Hybrid IT Footprint**

Gain visibility into the actual versus
intended mix of on-premises, private cloud, and public cloud environments. Track TCO trends
and identify misalignment between migration strategy and execution.

• **Hybrid IT:
Evaluate Migration Financial Benefit**

Assess whether migrated applications are
delivering the expected cost savings. Identify applications with increased TCO or unit costs
post-migration to guide corrective action.

• **Hybrid IT: Detailed Pre- and
Post-Migration Analysis**

Compare application-level financials before and after
migration. Pinpoint key cost drivers across IT towers and cost pools to validate migration
business cases and investment decisions.

## **Outcomes**

• Clear visibility into the financial impact of application migrations across hybrid
environments

• Validation of migration business cases using defensible TCO and unit cost metrics

• Early identification of migration inefficiencies and cost regressions

• Improved decision-making for future hybrid IT and cloud migration investments

• Stronger alignment between migration strategy, execution, and financial outcomes
