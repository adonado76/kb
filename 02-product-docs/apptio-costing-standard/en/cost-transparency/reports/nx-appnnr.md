# New and Retired Apps

Use this report to track changes in your application portfolio, including new
additions and retirements over time, identifying trends in how the landscape evolves. Access details
such as ownership, business criticality, and service dates to better understand periods of
significant change.

This report is designed for use by the following personas:

- CIO
- Application Portfolio Managers
- Enterprise Architects
- IT Financial Controllers
- Business Unit Leaders

## Key Elements

![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-appnnr.png)

![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-appnnr1.png)

| Element | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Filter controls (1) | Three filters let you narrow the report by application type, application IT owner, application family, and application business owner. |
| Summary Cards (2) | Four summary cards show application count, percentage count change year over year, new applications over 12 months, and retired applications over 12 months. |
| New and Retired Apps Trend Chart (3) | A line chart shows the trend over time for new applications and retired applications. |
| New Apps Over 12 Months Details Table (4) | This table includes columns such as application name, application ID, application family, application function, business criticality, application business owner, application IT owner, and application in-service date. |
| Retired Apps Over 12 Months Details Table (5) | This table includes columns such as application name, application ID, application family, application function, business criticality, application business owner, application IT owner, and application retirement date. |

## Questions Answered

- Is our application portfolio growing or shrinking?
- How many new applications did we add in the last 12 months?
- How many applications did we retire in the last 12 months?
- What types of applications are we adding (by family and function)?
- Who owns the new and retired applications?
- When were specific applications added or retired?
- What is the trend of new versus retired apps over time?

## Recommended Actions

- Review the trend chart to identify months with high application additions and understand what
  drove those increases.
- Compare new apps (12) versus retired apps (11) to assess if portfolio rationalization efforts
  are on track.
- Filter by Application Family to see which areas (Sales and Marketing, Finance, HR, etc.) are
  adding or removing the most applications.
- Check the Business Criticality of new apps to ensure you're investing in Mission Critical or
  Business Essential applications.
- Review retired applications to confirm they were properly decommissioned and costs were
  eliminated.
- Click on application names to view detailed information about each new or retired
  application.
- Use the Application IT Owner filter to see which teams are responsible for the most portfolio
  changes.
