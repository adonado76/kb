# Labor Analysis

Use this report to analyze labor costs and headcount by cost center, location, role type,
and vendor, comparing internal employees with external contractors and planned versus actual
headcount.

This report is designed for use by the following personas:

- Financial Analysts
- Cost Center Owners
- Resource Managers
- HR Teams
- Procurement Teams

## Key Elements

![](../../../../../03-media/apptio-costing-standard/resources/images/ct_images/nx-labanalysis.png)

| Element | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Global Slicers (1) | Seven filters let you narrow the report by region, cost center and name, cost center owner, owner, employee type, vendor name, and role type. |
| Labor Interactive Analysis Table (2) | This table includes columns such as cost center and name, location, role type, vendor name, headcount, headcount plan, internal headcount, external headcount, and operating expense. |
| Additional Details Panel (3) | This panel lets you show or hide available columns, including financial metrics, headcount metrics, rate metrics, and dimensional attributes. |
| Drill-down Links (4) | Cost center names link to more detailed information for the selected cost center. |

## Questions Answered

- What is the split between internal employees and external contractors across cost centers?
- Which vendors are providing most of the workforce and how much are we spending on them?
- How is our workforce distributed across different locations?
- Which roles or cost centers have the highest labor costs?
- How does actual headcount compare with planned headcount?
- Are there differences in costs or rates across vendors, roles, or locations?
- Where are we over or under planned headcount and where can we optimize costs or resources?

## Recommended Actions

After analyzing the Labor Analysis report, take these recommended actions:

- Identify high-cost areas by reviewing cost centers or vendors with the highest labor spend.
- Analyze the ratio of internal and external headcount to understand dependence on contractors and
  identify areas for conversion.
- Review headcount against plan to identify overstaffing or shortages and update workforce plans
  accordingly.
- Compare labor costs across locations to find opportunities for cost optimization.
- Review vendor usage to identify possible consolidation or cost-saving opportunities.
- Drill down into detailed data to investigate specific cost or headcount variances.
- Review the report regularly to track trends and ensure actions are followed through.
