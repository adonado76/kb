# Cost Take-Out Analytics &amp; Insights

## Overview

Cost take-out reports are out-of-the-box (OOTB) reports that consolidate other IBM Apptio
reports, making it easier to

- Identify opportunities to reduce and optimize costs across
  - [Vendors](../reports/vendor-cost.html)
  - [Applications](../reports/application-cost.html)
  - [Labor](../reports/labor-cost.html)
- Enable faster, data-driven decisions.

## Problem

IT and Finance teams face difficulties in identifying high-impact cost reduction opportunities in
IBM Apptio due to fragmented data and lack of targeted, cost reduction reports. They require clear
visibility into cost drivers to optimize vendor, application, and labor spend.

## Solution

**[Vendor Cost Take-Out
Reports](../reports/vendor-cost.html)**

Optimize your vendor portfolio. Identify high-spend or overlapping vendors by sorting and
prioritizing on annual run-rate. Pivot by function, tower, or cost pool to spot consolidation
opportunities, drill into linked apps and business units for context.

- Should we shift work towards or away from any vendors?
- Can we re-negotiate any contracts?
- Are there any vendors we should not renew our contracts with?

**[Application Cost Take-Out
Reports](../reports/application-cost.html)**

Streamline and rationalize your application portfolio. Compare application annual run-rates YoY
to find redundancy or low-value apps. Pivot by function or family, plan phased migrations, factor in
depreciation, and document retirements or consolidations alongside related vendor or labor
actions.

- What is the average cost per user of each application?
- Are we paying for redundant or duplicate applications that are serving the same purpose?
- We have this application's contract expiring soon – should we renew it?

**[Labor Cost Take-Out
Reports](../reports/labor-cost.html)**

Support strategic workforce planning and hiring decisions. Identify high-cost roles or teams,
vendor duplication across locations. View the potential cost savings of sourcing labor resources
through lower-cost locations and vendors.

- How much can we save by hiring an Engineer in Seattle vs Chicago?
- Should we employ an internally or outsource?
- Which vendor gets us the lowest rates?

To know more about configuration, see [Cost Take-Out Reports Configuration Guide](cost-takeout-config-guide.html)
