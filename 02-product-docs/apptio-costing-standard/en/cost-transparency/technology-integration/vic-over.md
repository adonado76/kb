# Vendor Insights - Coupa Integration Overview

## Overview

Vendor Insights - Coupa Integration enables organizations to govern technology spend across
procurement and finance by integrating Coupa Business Spend Management (BSM) with IBM
Apptio. Through CoupaLink, technology purchases are categorized at purchase order (PO)
inception using IBM Apptio’s ATUM® taxonomy and later reconciled with General Ledger data.
This provides early visibility into technology spend, reduces manual categorization effort
for IT Finance, and surfaces ungoverned technology spend occurring outside traditional IT
cost centers.

By combining procurement and financial data, this capability helps organizations improve
cost transparency, strengthen spend governance, and proactively manage budgets across IT and
non-IT business units.

## Persona

• **IT Finance**

Gains early visibility into technology spend, reduces manual GL categorization effort, and
improves budget governance.

• **Procurement / Vendor Managers**

Tracks purchase orders and contracts to prevent duplicate spend, control vendor costs, and
enforce procurement policies.

• **Cost Center Owners**

Monitors technology spend against budgets to avoid overruns and improve
accountability.

## Usecases

• **Govern Ungoverned Technology Spend**

Capture and categorize technology spend at PO creation across all business units to
surface spend outside IT-controlled budgets.

• **Purchase Order & Contract Alignment**

Track and reference purchase orders against vendor contracts and IT budgets to prevent
duplication and unplanned spending.

• **Proactive Spend Planning & Forecasting**

Leverage procurement data to forecast technology spend, update budgets automatically, and
monitor trends against plan.

• **Vendor Contract Oversight**

Monitor contract timelines and obligations to support renegotiation, retire unnecessary
agreements, and reduce financial risk.

## Outcomes

- Early and consistent visibility into technology spend at the point of purchase across
  the enterprise.
- Reduced manual effort and errors in monthly spend categorization and
  reconciliation.
- Improved control over unplanned, duplicate, or off-budget technology purchases.
- Stronger alignment between procurement, finance, and IT through standardized TBM-based
  categorization.
- Enhanced budget forecasting accuracy and proactive management of technology spend.
- Better vendor and contract governance, supporting cost optimization and risk
  reduction.
