# Business Unit Overview

## Overview

The Business Units capability provides comprehensive visibility into how technology costs
are consumed across different business units within the organization. It enables allocation
of costs from Business Services using both direct and indirect allocation methods, helping
business units understand their total technology spend in a transparent and defensible
manner.

By consolidating costs from applications, services, and projects, this capability presents
a complete view of IT resource consumption at the business unit level. It supports showback
and chargeback initiatives, improves cost accountability, and helps business leaders make
informed decisions about technology investments. Standardized allocation methodologies
ensure consistent and fair distribution of both consumption-based and overhead costs across
the enterprise.

## Personas

• **Business Unit Leaders** - Own overall technology consumption for their business
units and use cost transparency to guide funding decisions, prioritization, and
accountability.

• **Department Leaders**- Review department-level technology usage and costs to manage
budgets, identify inefficiencies, and align IT consumption with operational needs.

• **Business Relationship Managers** - Use business unit cost views to communicate IT
value, support demand discussions, and align technology spend with business outcomes.

• **IT Finance** - Designs and governs allocation methodologies, reviews business unit
technology spend, and supports showback and chargeback discussions.

• **Solution Owners**- Understand how their services and solutions are consumed by
different business units and identify opportunities for cost optimization.

## Use Cases

**Supported Out-of-the-box**

- **TCO Showback**
- Provide a comprehensive view of consumed services, including total costs, volumes,
  users, and unit costs, to support informed investment decisions and constructive cost
  discussions.
- **Flexible Allocation Methods and Outcomes**
- Explain and apply different service cost allocation methods, including direct and
  indirect allocations, to improve allocation accuracy, transparency, and stakeholder
  trust.
- **Drill and Trace of Costs**
- Illustrate how costs flow through the cost model layers, from infrastructure to
  applications to services, to provide deeper insight into cost drivers and allocation
  logic.

**Custom use cases:**

- **Classify Consumption**
- Track technology costs and usage by business capability or business process to improve
  visibility into consumption patterns and identify optimization opportunities.

## Outcomes

• Clear visibility into total technology spend by business unit and departments.

• Consistent and defensible allocation of direct and indirect IT costs.

• Improved transparency for showback and chargeback initiatives.

• Better understanding of consumption patterns across services and applications.

• Stronger alignment between technology spend and business value.
