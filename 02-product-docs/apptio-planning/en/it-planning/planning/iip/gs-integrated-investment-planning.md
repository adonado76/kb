# Project Planning Overview

Important: *Available with the* ***Apptio Planning Standard****subscription*

## Overview

Project Planning in Apptio Planning—also known as Integrated Investment Planning (IIP)—
enables IT organizations to understand the total cost and financial impact of their
technology investments. Whether investments are managed as projects, portfolio epics, or
value streams, Apptio Planning provides a unified view of all costs across the investment
lifecycle.

By planning investment costs from both Build and Run perspectives, IT leaders can evaluate
the full Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), ensure financial accountability, and forecast the
long-term operational impacts of strategic initiatives.

Project Planning in Apptio Planning allows IT leadership to gain in-depth insight into the
true cost of investments and their effect on overall IT budgets, while tracking individual
investment performance and adjusting plans in real time.

## Key Capabilities

**Integrated Expense Planning**

Easily link any budget line item to a planned or existing investment. This ensures that all
spend—whether CapEx or OpEx—is connected to a strategic initiative, providing a consolidated
view of technology investments across the enterprise.

**Labor Activity Planning**

Allocate both internal and external resources to investments using role-based or name-based
planning. Support for cross-charging allows costs to be accurately transferred between
departments, ensuring consuming organizations reflect the true cost of effort.

**Build and Run Cost Planning**

Plan the full lifecycle costs of each investment, from initial delivery (Build) to ongoing
operations (Run). This dual perspective allows organizations to anticipate future
operational budgets and better forecast the downstream financial impact of today’s
initiatives.

**Role-Based Access and Collaboration**

Control visibility and edit access for projects through user permissions that allow
different personas—such as Product Managers, Project Managers, and PMO teams—to contribute
directly to the planning process. This collaborative model ensures each stakeholder can
view, update, and approve project data relevant to their scope of responsibility, improving
coordination between finance and delivery teams.
