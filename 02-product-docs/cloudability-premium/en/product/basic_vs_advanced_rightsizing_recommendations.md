# Basic vs Advanced Rightsizing Recommendations

Cloudability Premium customer have access to a breadth of recommendations provided by both
Cloudability and Turbonomic’s rightsizing engines. These engines provide two different methods of
optimization along with different ways of customization those recommendations. This page details the
difference along with common questions from our users.

**Basic Recommendations** are the Cloudability rightsizing recommendations provided by
Cloudability across a breadth of service and are available to all customers regardless of SKU.

**Advanced Recommendations** are the recommendations generated by Turbonomic’s rightsizing
engine and only available to Cloudability Premium users.

## Understanding the difference between Basic and Advanced Recommendations

With two different engines and approaches to optimization, it is expected that the
recommendations would be different. Organizations must decide which recommendation set best fits
their goals and processes. The best engine for an organization may be different for different teams
or different services depending on how teams collaborate or operate.

**Basic Recommendations** are based on the idea of options for FinOps teams and Engineering
teams to review and consider before deciding what path to take.

Cloudability provides often multiple options for a target resource types along with a risk score
and other data. This wide range of options that meet the optimization criteria help customers
understand different paths they can take, along with potential trade offs to facilitate further
discussion and decision making based on context that Cloudability’s optimization engine may not have
insight into.

**Advanced Recommendations** are designed to be highly customizable and automatable. You’d
never want to automate recommendations that you can’t tailor to your business, your constraints, and
your priorities so the Advanced Recommendations (powered by Turbonomic) offer you that flexibility.
Advanced Recommendations allow for you to tune those recommendations for your specific business
needs and constraints through a flexible and powerful policy engine. These policies allow you to
codify the constraints or limitations that may impact what actions you could take versus others that
may not be realistic or desired for your business.

At scale in your organization, these policies should operate as an opportunity and mechanism for
FinOps teams and engineering teams to collaborate and agree on constraints and criteria for
optimization. With that agreement and context established up front, your advanced recommendations
will be actionable and tailored to your needs so users can focus on execution and implementation
instead of investigation and other considerations.

## FAQs

**My Basic and Advanced Recommendations don’t match – is that expected?** It is expected.
These two different approaches (detailed above) will provide different results and offer different
ways of thinking about or quantifying optimization opportunities. Organizations should think about
the way they like to operate and consider with recommendation set works best for them for a given
cloud services (as not all services are present in both sets of recommendations).

**When I look at other places in Cloudability showing rightsizing recommendations, what
recommendations engine is being surfaced?**

- **Rightsizing Page –** Both sets of recommended actions are available under their respective
  tabs on the Rightsizing Page
- **Rightsizing ROI –** Rightsizing ROI only supports Cloudability’s native (Basic)
  recommendations
- **Optimization Dashboard –** User can select if they want to display the rightsizing recs or
  the
- **Apptio BI –** Apptio BIonly supports Cloudability’s native (Basic) for a limited set of
  services/lookback periods.

**I’ve decided I only want to see the Advanced set, can I hide the basic recommendations to
avoid any questions?** Yes, you can! You can find this setting on the Rightsizing preferences
page, under the advanced section.

**Can I unify these sets of recommendations into a single view?** Not yet, but stay tuned
