# Report Collections — Resource Towers Layer

This brief documents the report collections tied to the Resource Towers layer: IT Towers, Infra & Cloud, Data Centers, Compute, Storage, and Hybrid IT. Mainframe NX Reports (the richest collection in this layer) are documented separately in the Mainframe capability brief.

## IT Towers Report Collection

Delivers cost-per-unit visibility to see total cost and efficiency trends according to the standard ATUM taxonomy, aligned with peer benchmarks — with an automated model showing how general-ledger budget variance impacts each tower/function. Four reports: IT Towers Review, IT Towers Performance, IT Towers Composition, and IT Towers Planning.

## Infra & Cloud Report Collection

Delivers automated data feeds from public cloud providers, aggregating and translating billing detail into standard IT categories — enabling a fully-burdened public cloud TCO view in the same report as on-premises assets. One report: Summary, including Server and Storage.

## Data Center Report Collection

Compares cost, capacity, and utilization across data centers, with actionable KPIs to isolate and understand anomalous spend. Aimed at Director of IT, Head of Operations, and DC Operations Manager. Answers which data centers cost less per unit of operation and what capacity is available in each, with cost-per-kilowatt and cost-per-square-foot metrics.

## Compute Cost Overview Collection

Delivers cost, capacity, and utilization of Compute resources — spend profile, operational metrics, server utilization, and a detailed server (host and logical) application view. Aimed at Director of IT, the Compute team, Head of Operations, and IT Service Owners. Answers which server platforms require additional unit purchases, what the cost per server-hour is for each application, which platforms should be retired, and where opportunities exist to migrate from on-premises to public cloud.

## Storage Cost Overview Collection

Delivers cost, capacity, and utilization of Storage resources — spend profile, storage metrics, device and logical unit detail, and a storage application view. Aimed at Director of IT, the Compute team, Head of Operations, and Service Owners. Answers whether storage tiers are aligned with the business criticality of the application portfolio, which units are approaching end-of-life, the cost of unallocated capacity, average and effective unit storage cost, and where opportunities exist to reclaim underutilized capacity.

## Hybrid IT Collection

The most granular collection in this layer, with three individually documented reports, each with its own personas and insights:

**Hybrid IT TCO Impact – Summary**: high-level view of the hybrid environment, comparing current and historical state of on-premises, private cloud, and public cloud deployments. Aimed at CIOs and C-Suite executives, Application Owners, and IT Finance/Leadership. Shows total TCO, application count, and average cost per application across the three environments, compares current vs. target hybrid footprint, and analyzes the hybrid mix by business criticality, investment objective, and application family.

**Hybrid IT TCO Impact – Insights**: detailed analysis of the financial impact of application migrations, comparing TCO, unit cost, and volume before and after. Aimed at C-Suite executives, Application Owners, and IT Finance. Identifies applications where TCO or unit cost increased after migration, analyzes the distribution by migration type (On-Prem to Public Cloud, Public Cloud to Private Cloud, etc.) and strategy (Rehost, Refactor, Rebuild, Replatform), and tracks how many applications deliver financial benefit vs. deterioration.

**Hybrid IT TCO Impact – Analysis**: detailed application-level view of the financial drivers behind migration outcomes. Aimed at Application Owners and IT Finance. Compares pre- and post-migration financial drivers, identifies which Cost Pools (labor, fixed assets, vendors) drive increases or savings, and understands the impact of IT Towers and sub-towers as resource drivers behind the migration outcome.

## Known limitation

The five collections other than Hybrid IT (IT Towers, Infra & Cloud, Data Centers, Compute, Storage) do not have screenshots available in the documentation cut normalized for this knowledge base — they are documented by purpose, report contents, and business questions answered, without image support. Hybrid IT and Mainframe NX (see the Mainframe capability brief) are the two collections in this layer with real screenshots.
