# Technology Business Model Archetype: Value Partner — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Supports This Model

## The archetype

Value Partner organizations are "more ingrained in the business than most service provider
organizations are" — trusted enough to co-own decisions about bespoke applications and integrated
solutions, and increasingly to lead a company's digital agenda. The TBM Council's description of
how Value Partners implement each discipline shifts the unit of analysis up a level, from services
to business capabilities:

- **Creating Transparency**: "Value partners know the total cost of delivering business
  application services, including those on a per-unit basis. They often know the total cost of
  delivering business capabilities... so they and their business partners can decide on tradeoffs."
- **Delivering Value for Money**: "Tech leaders monitor the unit costs of business application
  services. They balance tech spending across their portfolio and make tradeoffs to improve value."
- **Shaping Business Demand**: "Technology costs are evaluated as business costs. Investments in
  technology are based on the business impact on product-line P&Ls."
- **Planning and Governing for Value**: "The budget is based on business plans that have a clear
  connection between innovation and service demands and IT resource needs."

## How IBM Apptio Costing Standard addresses each discipline

This is where the **Application TCO** and **Product TCO** capabilities (both already documented in
this knowledge base) become the primary tools, not the Services capability alone:

- **Application TCO** delivers exactly the "total cost of delivering business application
  services... on a per-unit basis" the archetype requires, with the added dimension of lifecycle
  management (invest, tolerate, migrate, retire) that ties directly into portfolio tradeoff
  decisions.
- **Product TCO** goes further, matching the archetype's shift toward evaluating "technology costs
  as business costs" — its explicit design goal of supporting "product roadmap, funding, and
  lifecycle decisions" for Product Managers and Executives directly mirrors "investments... based
  on the business impact on product-line P&Ls."
- The **Business Unit** capability's persona list — which names Business Relationship Managers
  explicitly as users who "communicate IT value, support demand discussions, and align technology
  spend with business outcomes" — is the operational home for the BRM function the archetype
  depends on.
- **IT Financials'** Budget Variance Analysis, extended with Application and Product TCO data,
  supports the "clear connection between innovation and service demands and IT resource needs" the
  archetype's planning discipline requires.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

The TBM Council frames Value Partner as "the most indispensable model for any purely 'IT'
organization" — the level at which a CIO earns "a seat at the table" because they understand not
just IT, but how it applies to the business. That's a meaningfully higher bar than Service
Provider, and it shows up directly in which Costing Standard capabilities matter most: Application
and Product TCO (business-capability-level cost) rather than just Service TCO (service-level
cost).

For a client aspiring to Value Partner status, the honest scoping conversation is that Product TCO
in particular assumes some organizational maturity already exists — it's explicitly built for
organizations "shift[ing] from service-based delivery to product-centric operating models," which
is itself a business transformation, not just a tooling change. Costing Standard can support that
shift, but it doesn't substitute for the underlying organizational change the archetype requires.
