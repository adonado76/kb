# Vendor Management — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

Vendor spend is one of the fastest paths to demonstrating TBM value, precisely because it
doesn't require the full allocation model to be built first. An organization can typically pull
vendor invoice/contract data early, see spend concentration and fragmentation immediately, and
act on it — no need to wait for labor mapping, tower allocation, or application TCO to mature
first. That makes it one of the better "quick win" capabilities in a phased TBM adoption.

## How IBM Apptio Costing Standard covers it

Costing Standard's Vendor capability is explicitly scoped: "This capability focuses on vendor
spend analysis only. For deeper visibility into contracts, purchase orders, or accounts payable,
refer to the Vendor Insights reports, which extend vendor analysis with commercial and
transactional views" — a useful distinction to set expectations correctly in a proposal (spend
analysis is in scope from day one; contract/PO-level detail is a deeper, separate capability).

Seven out-of-the-box use cases are available immediately:

- **Top Vendor Spend Analysis** — surface the vendors worth renegotiating or consolidating.
- **Track & Eliminate Vendors with the Least Spend** — the mirror image: find fragmentation
  (dozens of low-spend vendors doing overlapping things) and clean it up.
- **Strategic Vendor Classification & Rationalization** — classify vendors as strategic,
  preferred, or transactional to focus relationship investment where it matters.
- **Vendor Spend Visibility** — baseline transparency for budgeting and financial oversight.
- **Vendor Consolidation & Rationalization** — identify overlapping vendor capabilities.
- **Vendor Cost Allocation** — carry vendor cost through to cost centers, projects, and solution
  offerings, connecting vendor spend to the rest of the TCO picture once that model matures.
- **Vendor Cost Analysis & Benchmarking** — compare spend patterns across vendors or regions to
  support negotiation.

## Why this matters in a client conversation — the early-adoption value story

This is a strong answer to the most common early-adoption objection: "TBM sounds valuable, but
it'll be six months before we see anything." Vendor spend concentration analysis (top vendor
spend, least-spend vendor elimination, strategic classification) can be stood up on invoice and
contract data alone — it doesn't require the labor workbench, tower mapping, or a mature cost
model to already be in place. A client can walk away from an early phase with a concrete
renegotiation or consolidation target list, funded by real savings, well before the full TBM
Model is operational.

That makes Vendor Management a natural **first capability** in a phased rollout — it builds
organizational trust in the platform (and in the practice) with a fast, visible win, while the
foundational allocation work (Cost Pools → Resource Towers, covered in earlier briefs) proceeds
in parallel for the longer-term TCO capabilities.

The persona spread also matters here: Vendor Manager sits alongside IT Finance, Cost Center
Owner, and Service Owner as a named persona — this capability is designed to be directly useful
to procurement-adjacent roles, not just financial analysts, widening the internal coalition that
benefits from (and can champion) the TBM initiative early.
