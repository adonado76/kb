# Network Devices and Communications — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## Network Devices: the capability

Network Devices addresses a predictability problem: network and communications costs are difficult to anticipate and control, with unexpected increases typically originating from downtime, bandwidth, or over-specified software, underutilized links, and poor visibility into network asset lifecycle.

Four personas anchor this capability: IT Finance, IT Leaders, Service Owners, and Network and Infrastructure Owners. Its out-of-the-box use case, Network Utilization and Efficiency, monitors lines, contracts, and network utilization to optimize communications cost while maintaining reliable service levels.

## Communications: a narrower, complementary capability

Communications is a more focused capability that complements Network Devices: it gives foundational transparency into communications infrastructure cost (circuits, voice, usage-based charges), allocating it to the appropriate consumers. Three personas anchor it: IT Finance, Service Owner, and IT Operations. Its out-of-the-box use case, Communication Cost Allocation, allocates communications infrastructure cost toward servers, end-user devices, and business services, establishing a defensible application and service TCO.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

Together, these two capabilities close a gap that often goes unaddressed in early TBM engagements: network and communications spend is real, recurring, and often poorly attributed to the services that actually depend on it. Network Devices gives the utilization/efficiency lens; Communications gives the allocation lens that feeds directly into Application and Service TCO — the two are complementary, not redundant.
