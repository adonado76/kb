# Storage — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

Storage solves a very specific visibility problem: many organizations cannot distinguish allocated capacity from actually-used capacity across thousands of devices, which produces over-provisioning, inefficient tiering, and higher-than-necessary storage cost.

Four personas anchor this capability: IT Finance, Service Owners, Infrastructure and Storage Owners, and FinOps/CCoE. Four out-of-the-box use cases: Storage Usage and Efficiency (identify underutilized capacity by platform, tier, and device), Optimize Hybrid Infrastructure (communicate right-sizing benefits to application owners), Track and Communicate Hybrid Infrastructure Cost Savings, and Hybrid Infrastructure TCO (compare cost between on-premises and public cloud).

## Two tiers of the same capability

The same two-tier pattern repeats here: **Storage Cost Overview** delivers a foundational view (addressable vs. used capacity, high-level storage economics, Infrastructure & Cloud Summary and Storage TCO reports), while **Storage Insights & Optimization** delivers deeper operational analytics to answer whether storage tiers are aligned with application criticality, which assets are approaching end-of-life, how much unallocated or underutilized capacity exists, and what the effective unit cost of total vs. allocated storage actually is.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

Storage tiering misalignment (critical applications on cheap tiers, or low-priority data on expensive tiers) is one of the most common and most fixable sources of infrastructure waste — and it's a finding that Storage Insights & Optimization surfaces directly, without requiring the full Application TCO model to be built first. This makes Storage, like Vendor Management and Compute, a credible early-value capability in an adoption roadmap.
