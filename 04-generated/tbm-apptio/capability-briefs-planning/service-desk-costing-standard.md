# Service Desk — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

Service Desk addresses a connection problem: many organizations have fragmented visibility into service demand and struggle to clearly connect service desk cost to business outcomes, while facing pressure to balance internal delivery against external providers like SaaS platforms.

Three personas anchor this capability: IT Finance, Service Desk Manager (oversees operations, ticket volumes, and performance metrics), and IT Service Owners. Three use cases: Service Desk Cost Transparency (understand total cost and its distribution by ticket type and category), Ticket Volume and Cost Analysis (analyze volumes and costs by severity, priority, category, location, and service), and Service Performance and Efficiency (track MTTR, cost per ticket, and trends).

## Two tiers of the same capability

As with Compute, Storage, and Public Cloud, two levels exist: **Service Desk Cost Overview** delivers a foundational view of cost and ticket volume through focused summary reports; **Service Desk Insights & Optimization** delivers deeper operational analytics by priority, severity, location, and service, with actionable KPIs and drill-down to prioritize improvement initiatives.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

Service Desk cost per ticket is one of the most intuitive unit-cost metrics to present to a non-technical business stakeholder — "it costs $X to resolve a ticket" is immediately understandable in a way that abstract infrastructure metrics are not. This makes Service Desk Cost Overview a strong candidate for an early transparency win, particularly for clients evaluating whether to insource or outsource service desk operations.
