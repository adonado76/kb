# Data Center TCO — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

Data center economics have gotten harder to reason about, not easier — AI workloads are
driving rack density up, hybrid architectures spread facilities across owned, leased, and
colocation sites, and rising power and cooling costs make the "true" cost of a facility a moving
target. Without unit-level visibility, decisions about consolidation, expansion, or migration get
made on incomplete facility data instead of defensible economics.

## How IBM Apptio Costing Standard covers it

IBM Apptio Data Center TCO consolidates financial, operational, and capacity data into a single
view spanning owned, leased, and colocation sites. Five use cases anchor it:

- **Data Center TCO & Unit Economics** — total cost, capacity, and power consumption across
  sites and facility types, expressed in defensible unit terms: cost per rack, cost per area, cost
  per kilowatt.
- **Capacity Utilization & Constraint Planning** — available vs. committed capacity across
  power, cooling, and space, to manage constraints proactively rather than discovering them during
  an expansion request.
- **Vendor, Labor & Contract Spend Transparency** — facility, labor, vendor, and contract cost
  analysis to strengthen procurement and contract management decisions.
- **Infrastructure Cost Allocation** — shared facility costs allocated down to compute and
  storage infrastructure, establishing defensible infrastructure unit costs.
- **Application & Service Cost Allocation** — facility costs carried all the way through to
  applications, products, and services, supporting showback/chargeback and business-aligned
  investment decisions.

Worth noting for anyone with prior Apptio exposure: the documentation explicitly states this is a
**unified, current solution** — "the previous Data Center Cost Overview and Data Center Insights &
Optimization solutions are deprecated and replaced by the unified IBM Apptio Data Center TCO
solution." Any client-facing material referencing those older solution names should be updated.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

This capability speaks directly to two audiences that don't usually read the same report: Data
Center and Facility Owners tracking power/cooling/space day to day, and CIO/CTO leadership making
multi-year consolidation or expansion bets. The unit economics framing (cost per rack, per kW) is
what lets those two conversations use the same numbers — a facility owner's operational metric
becomes the CFO's investment justification without translation.

For any client currently navigating AI infrastructure buildout or cloud repatriation — both named
explicitly as drivers of "increasing pressure" in the documentation — this is a timely,
evidence-backed capability to lead with, not a generic infrastructure costing pitch.
