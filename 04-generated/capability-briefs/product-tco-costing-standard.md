# Product TCO — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

As organizations shift from service-based IT delivery to product-centric operating models
(persistent teams organized around a product, not a project), the traditional TBM view — built
around Services and Applications — leaves a visibility gap. Product Managers and Portfolio
Leaders need to see cost the way their operating model actually works: by product, across its
full lifecycle, not just by the underlying service or application that happens to support it.

## How IBM Apptio Costing Standard covers it

Costing Standard's Product capability is explicit about the gap it closes: "Traditional
out-of-the-box costing and reporting models have primarily focused on services, creating a
visibility gap for organizations that need clear, defensible insight into product cost
structures and financial performance." Product TCO is the response — consistent, product-level
cost transparency layered on top of the same underlying allocation the rest of Costing Standard
uses.

Three use cases anchor this capability:

- **Product Cost and Driver Visibility** — continuous tracking of product costs and drivers
  (labor, vendors, tools, initiatives) to support accurate budgeting and forecasting.
- **Product Lifecycle and Investment Management** — resourcing and spend tracked across the
  full lifecycle, from ideation and development through maintenance and retirement, so investment
  can be rebalanced toward growth and innovation rather than staying static.
- **Customizable Product and Portfolio Hierarchies** — role-specific hierarchies so different
  stakeholders (an executive doing portfolio rollups, a PMO tracing project spend into a product)
  each get a view that fits how they think about the business, without breaking governance
  consistency underneath.

The persona list is broader than a typical costing capability: it explicitly includes Product
Managers and Portfolio Leaders, Executives and IT Leadership, and PMO/PPM Leaders alongside IT
Finance — a signal that this capability is meant to be read directly by product-organization
stakeholders, not translated for them by a costing analyst.

## Why this matters in a client conversation

This is the capability to lead with for any client actively transitioning — or already
operating — a product-centric delivery model (persistent product teams, agile funding, Value
Stream-aligned budgets). A client still organized around traditional Applications/Services will
get real value from the rest of Costing Standard without ever touching this; a client mid-way
through a product-operating-model transformation will specifically ask whether TBM "supports
product costing," and this is the direct, evidenced answer.

One explicit outcome worth quoting directly in a proposal: Costing Standard is built to "maintain
alignment across product-only and hybrid service delivery models" — meaning a client doesn't have
to fully complete their product-model transition before adopting this; it's designed to coexist
with organizations that are still partly organized by service.
