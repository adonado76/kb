# End User Devices — How IBM Apptio Costing Standard Addresses This TBM Capability

## The capability

End User Devices, as a Solution, focuses on the TCO of the workforce's computing equipment — PCs, laptops, smartphones, tablets, and other client compute equipment — giving visibility into device-related spend and enabling that cost to be allocated to the services that consume it.

## Two levels of maturity

In its foundational form, the capability has two personas (IT Finance and Service Owner) and one central use case: Cost Allocation to Services, which allocates device cost toward end-user services and specific business services.

The capability extends substantially as a standalone fleet management solution, with consolidated visibility into cost, inventory, and utilization of end-user compute assets. Four additional personas come into play: IT Asset Manager (manages inventory and lifecycle), Desktop Support/EUC Manager (oversees compliance and refresh planning), and the Service Owner of End User Services. Seven use cases: Fleet Spend Visibility (consolidate OpEx and CapEx of devices by cost center, service, and type), Device Cost Allocation (allocate cost toward End User Services and business services, enabling precise chargeback/showback), Device Age & Refresh Analysis (analyze lifecycle and estimated refresh cost), Inventory & Utilization Insights (visibility into allocation, usage patterns, and location to identify surplus capacity), Multiple & Orphan Device Identification (identify users with multiple devices or orphaned equipment for reclamation), Compliance Monitoring (track compliance with OS and hardware standards), and Optimization & Cost Reduction (standardize, consolidate, and right-size the fleet).

## Why this matters in a client conversation

The multiple/orphan device identification use case deserves particular mention in an early-adoption conversation: it's, alongside Vendor Management, another example of a quick win — it doesn't require the full allocation model to be mature, and produces a concrete, actionable list of devices to reclaim on the first pass of analysis.
